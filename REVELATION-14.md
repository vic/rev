## Revelation 14

---

### [Rev 14:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-1.htm) — "And I looked, and, lo, a Lamb stood on the mount Sion"

**Text**: "And I looked, and, lo, a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty and four thousand, having his Father's name written in their foreheads."

**Exegesis**: The opening of ch. 14 stands in deliberate contrast to ch. 13. While the majority of humanity has succumbed to the worldwide deception and rendered allegiance to the satanic trinity (ch. 13), John now beholds the victorious remnant. The structural contrast is exact: those who bear the mark of the beast (13:16–17) are here countered by those bearing the name of God and the Lamb.

**Mount Zion** (Gr. *Sion*): In the OT, Zion is the center of God's messianic rule. [Psalm 2:6](https://biblehub.com/psalms/2-6.htm): "I have installed my King on Zion, My holy mountain" (cf. Pss. 48:1–2; [Isa. 24:23](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/24-23.htm); [Mic. 4:7](https://biblehub.com/micah/4-7.htm)). Isaiah, Joel, and Obadiah use it as the site of final deliverance ([Isa. 59:20](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/59-20.htm); [Joel 2:32](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-32.htm); Obad. 17). In the NT it becomes "the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem" ([Heb. 12:22](https://biblehub.com/hebrews/12-22.htm)). John's vision fulfills [Joel 2:32](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-32.htm) precisely: "On Mount Zion and in Jerusalem there will be those who escape." Mount Zion in Revelation signifies the "eschatological victory of God" — the place of deliverance for God's faithful end-time people. Jerusalem in Revelation stands in deliberate contrast to Babylon.

**144,000**: This is the same group sealed in ch. 7 (see Notes on [Rev. 7:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-4.htm)). In ch. 7 they are sealed to be able to stand on the great day of God's wrath (cf. 6:17); subsequently seen standing before the throne (7:9); here they stand on Mount Zion. They are the remnant of the woman's offspring (12:17), the end-time sealed people who have passed through the final crisis and are now with Christ, "eternally secure, celebrating the great victory over the forces of darkness" (Stefanovic). The number 144,000 (12 × 12 × 1,000) denotes mathematical perfection and symmetry, symbolically designating the end-time true Israel — the "church militant" in its eschatological completeness.

**Father's name written in their foreheads**: Receiving a name on the forehead signifies "conformity to the character of Satan or God" (Stefanovic). This fulfills the promise to Philadelphia: "the name of my God and the name of the city of my God, the new Jerusalem" (3:12). It denotes eternal citizenship and wholehearted commitment to God — the direct counterpart to the beast's mark (13:16–17). Those with the beast's mark are "those who dwell on the earth" (13:14); the 144,000 are citizens of heaven.

**Cross-references**: Pss. 2:6; 48:1–2; [Isa. 24:23](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/24-23.htm); 59:20; [Mic. 4:7](https://biblehub.com/micah/4-7.htm); [Joel 2:32](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-32.htm); Obad. 17; [Heb. 12:22](https://biblehub.com/hebrews/12-22.htm); [Rev. 3:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/3-12.htm); 7:4; 9:4; 13:16–17; 22:4.

### [Rev 14:2](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-2.htm) — "And I heard a voice from heaven, as the voice of many waters"

**Text**: "And I heard a voice from heaven, as the voice of many waters, and as the voice of a great thunder: and I heard the voice of harpers harping with their harps:"

**Exegesis**: John hears the song of the 144,000 before he sees who sings it. The threefold description of the sound — many waters, great thunder, harpers — indicates overwhelming volume and sublime beauty combined. The same imagery recurs in [Rev. 19:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/19-1.htm), 6 when John hears the chorus of the redeemed in heaven: "their voices are like the sound of many waters and like the sound of great thunder." This intertextual link confirms that the singers here are the redeemed 144,000 themselves, not an angelic choir (as some commentators argue). The harps (*kithara*) are instruments of praise (cf. [Rev. 5:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/5-8.htm); 15:2). The sound of many waters evokes the voice of God himself in Ezekiel (1:24; 43:2) and Christ in [Rev. 1:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-15.htm) — the song of the redeemed participates in the very resonance of divine glory.

**Cross-references**: [Ezek. 1:24](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/1-24.htm); 43:2; [Rev. 1:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-15.htm); 5:8; 15:2–3; 19:1, 6.

### [Rev 14:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-3.htm) — "And they sung as it were a new song before the throne"

**Text**: "And they sung as it were a new song before the throne, and before the four beasts, and the elders: and no man could learn that song but the hundred and forty and four thousand, which were redeemed from the earth."

**Exegesis**: **"New song"** (*ode kaine*): Some commentators propose that the singers and harpers are angels with a message the 144,000 can only understand. Stefanovic rejects this: [Rev. 15:2](https://biblehub.com/revelation/15-2.htm)–3 plainly identifies the 144,000 as the harpers singing the Song of Moses and of the Lamb on the sea of glass. The "new song" there is explicitly attributed to the redeemed.

The new song is sung before the throne, the four living beings, and the twenty-four elders — the full court of heaven — as an act of cosmic worship. In [Revelation 15:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/15-3.htm) this new song is called "the song of Moses and of the Lamb," evoking Israel's celebratory song after the Exodus crossing of the Red Sea (Exod. 15). It is a song of deliverance and salvation. The 144,000 "have come from the great tribulation" (7:14); like Israel at the Red Sea, they now stand on the other side, celebrating a final deliverance. The song is uniquely theirs — no one else can learn it — because it is the song of redemption from an experience no other group in history has shared: passing through the ultimate final crisis, resisting the satanic trinity, and emerging victorious.

**"Redeemed"** (Gr. *agorazō*, "to buy" or "to purchase," [Rev. 3:18](https://biblehub.com/revelation/3-18.htm); 18:11; cf. [1 Cor. 6:20](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/6-20.htm)): The 144,000 belong to God as his purchased possession (cf. [Rev. 5:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/5-9.htm); [Rom. 3:24](https://biblehub.com/romans/3-24.htm)). They celebrate before his throne the redemption accomplished at Calvary.

**Cross-references**: Exod. 15; Pss. 33:3; 96:1; 149:1; [Isa. 42:10](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/42-10.htm); [Rev. 5:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/5-9.htm); 7:14; 15:2–4; 19:1, 6.

### [Rev 14:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-4.htm) — "These are they which were not defiled with women; for they are virgins"

**Text**: "These are they which were not defiled with women; for they are virgins. These are they which follow the Lamb whithersoever he goeth. These were redeemed from among men, being the firstfruits unto God and to the Lamb."

**Exegesis**: John identifies the 144,000 through a fourfold description.

**"Not defiled with women; virgins"**: This is symbolic, not literal celibacy. The Greek *molyno* ("to defile," aorist tense = action at a specific point in time) refers to the moment when the coalition of religious elements, symbolized by "women" (i.e., apostate religious systems), bring every pressure to bear on the saints to renounce loyalty to God and worship the beast — "any yielding would be an act of defilement" (SDA Bible Commentary). In Revelation, a faithful woman consistently symbolizes God's people (12:1; 19:7–8; 22:17), while a prostitute symbolizes the apostate and unfaithful (chs. 17–18). The plural "women" most likely refers to the great prostitute Babylon and her daughters (17:5). The 144,000 have refused to defile themselves by worshiping the beast; they have "kept themselves pure unto God." The Greek *parthenos* ("virgin") can apply to both sexes and is used metaphorically throughout the OT and NT for fidelity to God (cf. Israel called "virgin" when she returns to God: [2 Kgs. 19:21](https://biblehub.com/2_kings/19-21.htm); [Isa. 37:22](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/37-22.htm); [Jer. 18:13](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/18-13.htm); 31:4; [Lam. 2:13](https://biblehub.com/lamentations/2-13.htm); [Amos 5:2](https://biblehub.com/amos/5-2.htm)). Paul presents the Corinthian church as "a pure virgin to Christ" ([2 Cor. 11:2](https://biblehub.com/2_corinthians/11-2.htm)–3); the ten virgins in [Matt. 25:1](https://biblehub.com/matthew/25-1.htm)–13 await the Second Coming.

**"Follow the Lamb whithersoever he goeth"**: Entirely loyal to Christ, they put him first no matter what the cost. Having passed through the end-time deception victoriously, they now follow Christ eternally — "nobody and nothing is able to separate them from his love."

**"Firstfruits unto God and to the Lamb"** (Gr. *aparchē*): In the LXX, *aparchē* designates the firstfruits of the harvest offered in the sanctuary. Used figuratively, it denotes the redeemed set apart for God as his special offering. Jeremiah portrays redeemed Israel as "holy to the Lord, the firstfruits of his harvest" ([Jer. 2:3](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/2-3.htm)), distinct from oppressing nations now subject to judgment. James calls redeemed Christians "the firstfruits among his creatures" ([James 1:18](https://biblehub.com/james/1-18.htm)). The 144,000 as firstfruits must be understood in Jeremiah's sense: they are the firstfruits of the end-time grain harvest ([Rev. 14:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-14.htm)–16), redeemed and brought to God's sanctuary on Mount Zion, in contrast to the wicked who face the grape harvest of divine judgment (14:17–20). "They were redeemed" (*ēgorasthēsan*) = "release from slavery"; "redeemed from men" means they "no longer belong to the world" (Stefanovic).

**Cross-references**: [Jer. 2:3](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/2-3.htm); [2 Kgs. 19:21](https://biblehub.com/2_kings/19-21.htm); [Isa. 37:22](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/37-22.htm); [Jer. 14:17](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/14-17.htm); 18:13; 31:4; [Lam. 2:13](https://biblehub.com/lamentations/2-13.htm); [Amos 5:2](https://biblehub.com/amos/5-2.htm); [2 Cor. 11:2](https://biblehub.com/2_corinthians/11-2.htm)–3; [Matt. 25:1](https://biblehub.com/matthew/25-1.htm)–3; [James 1:18](https://biblehub.com/james/1-18.htm); [Rev. 5:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/5-9.htm); 14:14–16; 17:5.

### [Rev 14:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-5.htm) — "And in their mouth was found no guile: for they are without fault before the throne of God"

**Text**: "And in their mouth was found no guile: for they are without fault before the throne of God."

**Exegesis**: The 144,000 are characterized by two final attributes: truthfulness and blamelessness.

**"No guile"** (Gr. *pseudos*, "lie"): This is not merely ordinary untruthfulness; it is "the lying of the antichrist powers" ([1 [John 1:5](https://biblehub.com/john/1-5.htm)](https://biblehub.com/1_john/1-5.htm)–10; 2:21–22). Deception characterizes Satan's end-time activities ([2 Thess. 2:9](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-9.htm)–11; [Rev. 13:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-14.htm); 19:20) and the satanic trinity. Paul wrote of the ungodly who "exchanged the truth of God for a lie" ([Rom. 1:25](https://biblehub.com/romans/1-25.htm)); those who "refuse to receive the love of the truth" are deceived to believe "the lie" ([2 Thess. 2:10](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-10.htm)–11). The 144,000 hold firmly to the truth when the whole world is deceived. This fulfills Zephaniah's eschatological prophecy: "The remnant of Israel will do no wrong and tell no lies, nor will a deceitful tongue be found in their mouths" ([Zeph. 3:13](https://biblehub.com/zephaniah/3-13.htm)). Lying is a characteristic of those excluded from the New Jerusalem ([Rev. 21:27](https://biblehub.com/revelation/21-27.htm); 22:15).

**"Without fault"** (Gr. *amōmos*, "blameless"): In the LXX, *amōmos* is the technical term for sacrifices offered to God — unblemished and "sacrificially perfect." In the NT it describes Christ ([Heb. 9:14](https://biblehub.com/hebrews/9-14.htm); [1 Pet. 1:19](https://biblehub.com/1_peter/1-19.htm)) and the life Christians are called to before God ([Eph. 1:4](https://biblehub.com/ephesians/1-4.htm); 5:27; [Phil. 2:15](https://biblehub.com/philippians/2-15.htm); [Col. 1:22](https://biblehub.com/colossians/1-22.htm); Jude 24). For the 144,000, blamelessness does not mean sinless moral perfection but fidelity to Christ: "To be *amōmos* is not a quality unique to the 144,000, a state of perfection achieved only by those who go through the great tribulation. It is God's evaluation of all saints, Old Testament or New, who, like the 144,000, have 'washed their robes and made them white in the blood of the Lamb'" (Neall). To be blameless means to walk with God as Noah ([Gen. 6:9](https://biblehub.com/genesis/6-9.htm)) and Abraham ([Gen. 17:1](https://biblehub.com/genesis/17-1.htm)) did. Peter urges: "be diligent to be found by Him in peace, spotless and blameless" ([2 Pet. 3:14](https://biblehub.com/2_peter/3-14.htm)). They are found blameless in continuous relationship with Christ (cf. [Rom. 5:1](https://biblehub.com/romans/5-1.htm)).

**Cross-references**: [Zeph. 3:13](https://biblehub.com/zephaniah/3-13.htm); [1 [John 2:21](https://biblehub.com/john/2-21.htm)](https://biblehub.com/1_john/2-21.htm)–22; [2 Thess. 2:9](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-9.htm)–11; [Rom. 1:25](https://biblehub.com/romans/1-25.htm); [Gen. 6:9](https://biblehub.com/genesis/6-9.htm); 17:1; [Heb. 9:14](https://biblehub.com/hebrews/9-14.htm); [1 Pet. 1:19](https://biblehub.com/1_peter/1-19.htm); [Eph. 1:4](https://biblehub.com/ephesians/1-4.htm); 5:27; [Phil. 2:15](https://biblehub.com/philippians/2-15.htm); [Col. 1:22](https://biblehub.com/colossians/1-22.htm); Jude 24; [2 Pet. 3:14](https://biblehub.com/2_peter/3-14.htm); [Rev. 21:27](https://biblehub.com/revelation/21-27.htm); 22:15.

### [Rev 14:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-6.htm) — "And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel"

**Text**: "And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people,"

**Exegesis**: With the vision of the victorious 144,000 on Mount Zion complete, John turns to the proclamation that precedes it. Three angels flying in mid-heaven symbolically portray the worldwide proclamation of God's final warning. This is the fulfillment of Jesus' prediction: "This gospel of the kingdom shall be preached in the whole world as a testimony to all the nations, and then the end will come" ([Matt. 24:14](https://biblehub.com/matthew/24-14.htm)).

**"Those who dwell on the earth"** (*tous katoikountas epi tēs gēs*): In Revelation this phrase consistently denotes the worshipers of the beast — "those whose names are not written in the book of life" (13:8; cf. 6:10; 8:13; 11:10; 13:14). The final message is therefore directed specifically to *unbelievers*, those who have not yet sided with the beast, urging repentance before probation closes.

**"Everlasting gospel"** (Gr. *euangelion aiōnion*): The modifier "everlasting" or "eternal" is theologically significant: this is not a different gospel but the same unaltered gospel proclaimed since Pentecost. It is good news about Christ — his ministry, death, resurrection, heavenly enthronement, intercession, judgment, and imminent return. The "everlasting" quality indicates that this end-time gospel, while uniquely urgent for the closing period, is the same gospel Paul preached ([Gal. 1:8](https://biblehub.com/galatians/1-8.htm)–9). It is everlasting; Babylon is not.

**Universal scope**: "Every nation and tribe and tongue and people" — the fourfold formula echoing [Rev. 10:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/10-11.htm) where John is commissioned to prophecy "concerning many peoples and nations and tongues and kings." The commission given to John has further implication for the church. The worldwide scope of the beast's authority (13:7–8) is met with the worldwide scope of the gospel.

**Cross-references**: [Matt. 24:14](https://biblehub.com/matthew/24-14.htm); [Rev. 6:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/6-10.htm); 8:13; 10:11; 13:7–8; 14; [Gal. 1:8](https://biblehub.com/galatians/1-8.htm)–9.

### [Rev 14:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-7.htm) — "Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him"

**Text**: "Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come: and worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters."

**Exegesis**: The first angel's message consists of two exhortations based on a single ground.

**"Fear God"** (Gr. *phobeomai*, "to fear," "to reverence"): A well-known OT concept conveying the idea of taking God seriously in life by following him and obeying his commandments. When Israel saw God's power against Egypt, "the people feared him and believed" ([Exod. 14:31](https://biblehub.com/exodus/14-31.htm)). God's servants are consistently those "fearing God" ([Gen. 22:12](https://biblehub.com/genesis/22-12.htm); [1 Kgs. 18:3](https://biblehub.com/1_kings/18-3.htm); Pss. 22:23; 33:18; [Mal. 3:16](https://biblehub.com/malachi/3-16.htm); 4:2). "The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom" ([Ps. 111:10](https://biblehub.com/psalms/111-10.htm); [Prov. 1:7](https://biblehub.com/proverbs/1-7.htm)). Fearing God results in right doing ([Exod. 18:21](https://biblehub.com/exodus/18-21.htm); [Lev. 19:14](https://biblehub.com/leviticus/19-14.htm), 32) and avoiding evil ([Job 1:1](https://biblehub.com/job/1-1.htm); [Prov. 3:7](https://biblehub.com/proverbs/3-7.htm); 16:6). In Revelation's context, to fear God is to take him seriously, make a decisive turnaround, and be totally committed. Fearing God leads to repentance (16:9; cf. 11:13).

**"Give him glory"**: The aftereffect of fearing God — glorifying him through obedience to his commandments. Jesus: "By this is My Father glorified, that you bear much fruit" ([John 15:8](https://biblehub.com/john/15-8.htm)); "Jesus himself glorified the Father in accomplishing the work the Father gave him to do" ([John 17:4](https://biblehub.com/john/17-4.htm)). Christians give glory to God when they value themselves as God values them ([1 Cor. 6:19](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/6-19.htm)–20; 10:31). The exhortation echoes Moses' appeal to Israel before entering Canaan — to fear the Lord, obey him, keep his commandments ([Deut. 6:13](https://biblehub.com/deuteronomy/6-13.htm), 17; 10:12–13; 13:4; cf. 5:29; 6:2; 8:6; Eccl. 12:13).

**"The hour of his judgment is come"** (Gr. *krisis*, referring primarily to the *action* of judging, not the verdict — *krima* denotes the result; cf. [Rev. 16:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-7.htm); 18:10; 19:2): The verb "has come" is the ingressive aorist in Greek, pointing to the beginning of an action at a past point in time. This refers to the pre-advent investigative judgment taking place in heaven before the Second Coming — the first phase of judgment. The second phase (executive judgment on the wicked) occurs after the millennium ([Rev. 20:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/20-11.htm)–15). The conjunction *hoti* ("because/for") shows that the judgment's commencement is the *reason* for the exhortation to fear God, not merely a warning of future events (Beale). To God's faithful people this is good news — the time when God deals with all oppressing powers and vindicates his people (cf. the martyrs' cry: "How long?" 6:10). To the unfaithful it is a terror.

**"Worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters"**: The call to worship the Creator is a direct allusion to the fourth commandment: "In six days the Lord made the heavens and the earth, the sea and all that is in them, and rested on the seventh day; therefore the Lord blessed the Sabbath day and made it holy" ([Exod. 20:11](https://biblehub.com/exodus/20-11.htm)). The parallel between the first angel's language and the fourth commandment suggests that the call to worship God is given in the context of the Sabbath command. The central issue in the final crisis is *worship* — not denial of worship, but *who* is worshiped. [Revelation 14:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-7.htm) urges worship of the Creator-God while 13:4–8 describes the world worshiping the beast. The first angel's exhortation is the exact antithesis of the beast's demand. Paulien notes that the Sabbath will be "an integral part of the issue" in the final crisis (though not confined to it).

**Cross-references**: [Exod. 14:31](https://biblehub.com/exodus/14-31.htm); 18:21; 20:11; [Gen. 22:12](https://biblehub.com/genesis/22-12.htm); [Deut. 6:13](https://biblehub.com/deuteronomy/6-13.htm), 17; 10:12–13; Pss. 22:23; 33:18; 111:10; [Prov. 1:7](https://biblehub.com/proverbs/1-7.htm); Eccl. 12:13; [1 Kgs. 18:3](https://biblehub.com/1_kings/18-3.htm); [Mal. 3:16](https://biblehub.com/malachi/3-16.htm); 4:2; [John 15:8](https://biblehub.com/john/15-8.htm); 17:4; [1 Cor. 6:19](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/6-19.htm)–20; 10:31; [2 Cor. 5:10](https://biblehub.com/2_corinthians/5-10.htm); [Rev. 6:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/6-10.htm); 11:13; 13:4–8; 15:4; 16:9; 20:11–15; 22:12.

### [Rev 14:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-8.htm) — "And there followed another angel, saying, Babylon is fallen, is fallen"

**Text**: "And there followed another angel, saying, Babylon is fallen, is fallen, that great city, because she made all nations drink of the wine of the wrath of her fornication."

**Exegesis**: The second angel "follows" the first, indicating the messages are related and sequential — the second is the complement of the first. The first calls people back to the true God; the second declares the inevitable collapse of the rival system, Babylon, in order to move them to repudiate every scheme counter to allegiance to Christ (Johnsson).

**"Babylon is fallen, is fallen"**: The double announcement (past tense expressing future certainty — a prophetic perfect) emphasizes the imminence and inevitability of collapse. This echoes the exact words of the OT prophets: "Fallen, fallen is Babylon" ([Isa. 21:9](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/21-9.htm)); "Suddenly Babylon has fallen and been broken" ([Jer. 51:8](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/51-8.htm)). The historical fall of ancient Babylon (539 BC, when Cyrus the Persian diverted the Euphrates and captured the city during Belshazzar's drunken feast, Dan. 5) provides the typological background.

**"Babylon the great"**: In Revelation, Babylon is not a literal city but the end-time worldwide apostate religious confederacy made up of the satanic trinity (dragon, sea beast, earth beast — [Rev. 16:19](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-19.htm)), arrayed against God and his faithful people. Babylon "represents all human attempts to provide the way of salvation, all those plans and programs that, because they are built on human reason and devices, attempt to frustrate the divine plan for the world" (Johnsson). Like ancient Babylon, end-time Babylon exalts itself above God (cf. [2 Thess. 2:3](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-3.htm)–4; [Dan. 4:30](https://biblehub.com/daniel/4-30.htm)). It unites apostate religious organizations into the service of the satanic trinity. The Roman Babylon of the first century (cf. [1 Pet. 5:13](https://biblehub.com/1_peter/5-13.htm)) is an intermediate historical type. Its collapse, announced here, is elaborated in ch. 18.

**"The wine of the wrath of her fornication"**: This blends two OT images. First, ancient Babylon is portrayed as a cup of intoxicating wine: "Babylon has been a golden cup in the hand of the Lord, intoxicating all the earth. The nations have drunk of her wine; therefore the nations are going mad" ([Jer. 51:7](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/51-7.htm); cf. 25:15). Second, drinking Babylon's wine of fornication brings as consequence the drinking of God's wine of wrath (cf. [Job 21:20](https://biblehub.com/job/21-20.htm)). The imagery of "fornication/immorality" as a symbol of unfaithfulness to God pervades the OT ([Isa. 57:3](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/57-3.htm)–12; [Ezek. 16:15](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/16-15.htm), 26–29) and continues in the NT ([James 4:4](https://biblehub.com/james/4-4.htm); [Rev. 17:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-1.htm)–5). Babylon's seduction of all nations to side with the satanic trinity (ch. 13 — worship of the beast, reception of the mark) *is* the drinking of her wine of fornication. The inevitable consequence: those nations will drink of God's wrath (14:9–10).

**Cross-references**: [Isa. 21:9](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/21-9.htm); 13:19; [Jer. 25:15](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/25-15.htm); 51:7–8; [Job 21:20](https://biblehub.com/job/21-20.htm); [Ezek. 16:15](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/16-15.htm); [Dan. 4:30](https://biblehub.com/daniel/4-30.htm)–32; 5; [1 Pet. 5:13](https://biblehub.com/1_peter/5-13.htm); [James 4:4](https://biblehub.com/james/4-4.htm); [2 Thess. 2:3](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-3.htm)–4; [Rev. 13:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-11.htm)–17; 14:9–10; 16:19; 17:1–6; 18; 19:2.

### [Rev 14:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-9.htm) — "And the third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice"

**Text**: "And the third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice, If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive his mark in his forehead, or in his hand,"

**Exegesis**: A third angel with a warning message follows, building on the second. His warning is the most severe in Revelation and specifically concerns those who, rejecting the first angel's call to worship the true God, choose instead to worship the beast and receive his mark. The loud voice underscores urgency. The message is conditional — "if any man worship" — indicating that at this stage the choice is still open and the call to repentance still extends.

**The beast and his image**: Rev. 13 has established that the sea beast (the first beast) demands worship and that the earth beast compels humanity to make an image to the sea beast and enforces compliance with death (13:14–15). The "mark" (*charagma*) on the right hand or forehead (echoing 13:16) functions as the identification of the beast's worshipers — the exact counterpart to the seal of God on the forehead of the 144,000 (7:3; 9:4; 14:1). Worship of the beast and reception of his mark stands as the antithesis to obedience to God's commandments: "it has to do with the violation of the commandments of God" (Stefanovic). The mark of the beast and the seal of God are the two ultimate allegiance markers in the final crisis.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 7:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-3.htm); 9:4; 13:14–17; 14:1; 16:2; 19:20; 20:4.

### [Rev 14:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-10.htm) — "The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture"

**Text**: "The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation; and he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb:"

**Exegesis**: The third angel's warning announces the most terrifying consequence in Scripture.

**"The wine of the wrath of God"**: The execution of God's wrath symbolized by drinking wine from the Lord's cup pervades the OT: [Ps. 75:8](https://biblehub.com/psalms/75-8.htm); [Isa. 51:17](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/51-17.htm)–23; [Jer. 25:15](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/25-15.htm)–29; 49:12; [Ezek. 23:32](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/23-32.htm)–34; Obad. 16; [Job 21:20](https://biblehub.com/job/21-20.htm). [Psalm 75:8](https://biblehub.com/psalms/75-8.htm) is especially significant: "For a cup is in the hand of the Lord, and the wine foams; it is well mixed, and He pours out of this; surely all the wicked of the earth must drain and drink down its dregs." Jesus himself used this figure for his suffering on the cross ([Matt. 20:22](https://biblehub.com/matthew/20-22.htm); 26:39; [John 18:11](https://biblehub.com/john/18-11.htm)).

**Two Greek words for wrath**: *Thumos* ("anger," "fury") — a strong, passionate indignation; and *orgē* ("wrath") — a demonstration of displeasure and righteous indignation. *Orgē* is the usual Pauline word for divine wrath ([Rom. 1:18](https://biblehub.com/romans/1-18.htm); 3:5; 12:19; [Col. 3:6](https://biblehub.com/colossians/3-6.htm)). In Revelation both are used together for the eschatological wrath of God (14:10, 19; 15:1, 7; 16:1, 19; 19:15; cf. [Rom. 2:8](https://biblehub.com/romans/2-8.htm)) for intensification: this is wrath at its ultimate expression.

**"Without mixture"** (Gr. *akratos* — "undiluted," from *kerannymi*, "to mix"): In antiquity, wine was mixed with spices to increase potency, or with water to dilute it. "Mixed undiluted" is an oxymoron of intensification: this wine has been prepared at full strength without any dilution with water, i.e., without mercy. The final wrath of God is poured out in its full strength, not tempered with grace. This is the fulfillment of the seven last plagues (chs. 15–16) where "the wrath of God was completed" (15:1) and "no one was able to enter the temple" (15:8) because intercession has ceased.

**"Tormented with fire and brimstone"**: The language recalls "brimstone and fire from the Lord out of heaven" on Sodom and Gomorrah ([Gen. 19:24](https://biblehub.com/genesis/19-24.htm)) and Isaiah's prophecy about Edom's destruction ([Isa. 34:8](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/34-8.htm)–10). In Jude 7, Sodom and Gomorrah experienced "the punishment of eternal fire." The lake of fire burning with sulphur is the final destination of the beast and the false prophet (19:20), and all who sided with them (20:10).

**"In the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb"**: This points to the final judgment after the millennium (Rev. 20). The overcomers in Sardis are promised recognition "before my Father and his angels" (3:5); the unbelievers face their end before Christ and the angels. The parallel is exact and inverse.

**Cross-references**: [Ps. 75:8](https://biblehub.com/psalms/75-8.htm); [Isa. 51:17](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/51-17.htm)–23; 34:8–10; [Jer. 25:15](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/25-15.htm)–29; [Ezek. 23:32](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/23-32.htm)–34; [Gen. 19:24](https://biblehub.com/genesis/19-24.htm); Obad. 16; [Job 21:20](https://biblehub.com/job/21-20.htm); [Matt. 20:22](https://biblehub.com/matthew/20-22.htm); 26:39; [John 18:11](https://biblehub.com/john/18-11.htm); [Rom. 1:18](https://biblehub.com/romans/1-18.htm); 2:8; [Rev. 3:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/3-5.htm); 15:1; 16:1; 19:15; 19:20; 20:10.

### [Rev 14:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-11.htm) — "And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever"

**Text**: "And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever: and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name."

**Exegesis**: The verse carries two distinct ideas: the finality and completeness of the judgment, and the endless unrest of the beast-worshipers.

**"The smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever"**: The imagery is drawn directly from the OT. After God rained fire and sulphur on Sodom and Gomorrah, "Abraham saw the smoke ascending from the earth like the smoke of a furnace" ([Gen. 19:28](https://biblehub.com/genesis/19-28.htm)). Isaiah prophesied Edom's judgment with fire and sulphur: "it shall not be quenched night and day; its smoke shall go up forever; from generation to generation it shall be desolate" ([Isa. 34:8](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/34-8.htm)–10) — yet Edom does not burn forever in modern Jordan. The same pattern appears in [Rev. 19:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/19-3.htm) (Babylon's smoke ascending "for ever and ever") and 20:10. Stefanovic is explicit: "'Forever and ever' does not mean endless burning. No fire burns today in Sodom and Gomorrah and Edom in modern Jordan, although eternal fire was their allotment." Desmond Ford: "The purpose of fire is to consume, never to preserve." "The smoke ascending for ever and ever stands for burning which lasts long enough to make the consumption complete, with nothing left to be burned." The ascending smoke signals completion, not perpetuity.

**"They have no rest day nor night"**: This stands in sharp contrast to v. 13 which promises that those who die in the Lord "rest from their labors." The no-rest language echoes God's declaration to rebellious Israel: "Truly they shall not enter into My rest" ([Ps. 95:11](https://biblehub.com/psalms/95-11.htm)). The OT promise of rest in the land continues in the NT as rest in God's grace ([Heb. 4:9](https://biblehub.com/hebrews/4-9.htm)–11). "The rest into which God's people enter is eternal; so the unrest of the impending judgment for unbelievers is eternal" (Stefanovic). The grotesque language is intentional: to drive out the fear of the beast "by a stronger fear" (Stefanovic, echoing [Matt. 10:28](https://biblehub.com/matthew/10-28.htm)).

**Cross-references**: [Gen. 19:28](https://biblehub.com/genesis/19-28.htm); [Isa. 34:8](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/34-8.htm)–10; [Ps. 95:11](https://biblehub.com/psalms/95-11.htm); [Matt. 10:28](https://biblehub.com/matthew/10-28.htm); [Heb. 4:9](https://biblehub.com/hebrews/4-9.htm)–11; [Rev. 14:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-13.htm); 19:3; 20:10.

### [Rev 14:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-12.htm) — "Here is the patience of the saints"

**Text**: "Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus."

**Exegesis**: Verse 12 is John's appended comment to the three angels' messages, presenting the character of God's end-time people as the positive counterpoint to the fate of the beast's worshipers.

**"Here is the patience"** (Gr. *hypomonē*, "patient endurance," "steadfast perseverance"): The same phrase appears in [Rev. 13:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-10.htm) — "Here is the endurance and the faith of the saints." The saints of 14:12 are those of 13:7 against whom the satanic trinity wages war, identified in 12:17 as the remnant of the woman's offspring keeping "the commandments of God" and having "the testimony of Jesus." They are not called to resistance and protest but to endurance and perseverance. In the final crisis — "buffeted by false ideas, assaulted by religious confederacy, and threatened by the civil powers" (Stefanovic) — they remain firm.

**"Keep the commandments of God"**: The word *tēreō* ("to keep") means to "maintain" (cf. [2 Tim. 4:7](https://biblehub.com/2_timothy/4-7.htm): "I have kept the faith"). This is obedience to God's full law, including the Sabbath commandment implicitly referenced in v. 7. The mark of the beast "has to do with the violation of the commandments of God," while the seal of God is borne by those who "keep the commandments of God" (cf. 12:17; [Rev. 20:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/20-4.htm), 6, 12). The final crisis in Revelation 12–14 demonstrates "in the final crisis the commandments of God will emerge as a central issue" (Stefanovic).

**"Faith of Jesus"** (Gr. *pistis Iēsou*): This is the objective genitive — faith *in* Jesus, not faith belonging to Jesus. The same construction appears in [Rev. 2:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/2-13.htm) (cf. [Mark 11:22](https://biblehub.com/mark/11-22.htm); [James 2:1](https://biblehub.com/james/2-1.htm)). NEB: "remaining loyal to Jesus." To "keep" this faith means to "maintain" a living, ongoing relationship with Christ that sustains the saints "under the severe pressure of persecution" (Stefanovic). Nothing and no one can separate them from the love of God in Christ Jesus ([Rom. 8:38](https://biblehub.com/romans/8-38.htm)–39).

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 2:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/2-13.htm); 12:17; 13:7, 10; [Mark 11:22](https://biblehub.com/mark/11-22.htm); [James 2:1](https://biblehub.com/james/2-1.htm); [2 Tim. 4:7](https://biblehub.com/2_timothy/4-7.htm); [Rom. 8:38](https://biblehub.com/romans/8-38.htm)–39.

### [Rev 14:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-13.htm) — "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth"

**Text**: "And I heard a voice from heaven saying unto me, Write, Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth: Yea, saith the Spirit, that they may rest from their labours; and their works do follow them."

**Exegesis**: The second of the seven beatitudes of Revelation (1:3; 14:13; 16:15; 19:9; 20:6; 22:7, 14). A voice from heaven — likely Christ himself — commands John to write (the command *graphe* emphasizes the permanent and authoritative character of what follows), followed by a confirmation from the Holy Spirit.

**"Blessed"** (Gr. *makarios*): The same word used in the Beatitudes (Matt. 5). This blessing is particularly for those who "die in the Lord from henceforth" — i.e., from the time of the proclamation of the three angels' messages, the final crisis. These are the saints who die because of their faith and loyalty, apparently as martyrs or those who die before the Second Coming under the pressure of the final conflict.

**"Rest from their labours"** (Gr. *kopos* — "hard work," "labor to the point of weariness and exhaustion," cf. [Rom. 16:12](https://biblehub.com/romans/16-12.htm); [1 Cor. 15:10](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/15-10.htm); [Gal. 4:11](https://biblehub.com/galatians/4-11.htm); [1 Thess. 2:9](https://biblehub.com/1_thessalonians/2-9.htm)): This is not ordinary work but exhaustive, sacrificial gospel proclamation under persecution. These "labors" refer to the devotion of God's end-time saints to the gospel proclamation on behalf of which they suffered hardship and persecution to the point of death (cf. 12:11). The rest is "especially sweet after exhaustive, hard labor" (Stefanovic). This corresponds to the promise given to the martyred saints in the fifth seal scene — to "rest a little while longer" (6:11).

**"Their works do follow them"**: The "works" are the remnant's obedience to God in keeping his commandments and faith in Jesus (14:12). "Their endurance lies behind them, and they rest under God's watchful care until the hour of resurrection and coming glory" (Stefanovic). This is why Paul urges: "be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that your toil is not in vain in the Lord" ([1 Cor. 15:58](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/15-58.htm)). The contrast with v. 11 is stark: the wicked have no rest day or night; the saints rest from their labors.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 1:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-3.htm); 6:9–11; 12:11; [1 Cor. 15:58](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/15-58.htm); [Rom. 16:12](https://biblehub.com/romans/16-12.htm); [Gal. 4:11](https://biblehub.com/galatians/4-11.htm); [1 Thess. 2:9](https://biblehub.com/1_thessalonians/2-9.htm); [Rev. 6:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/6-11.htm); 14:11; 19:9; 20:6; 22:7, 14.

### [Rev 14:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-14.htm) — "And I looked, and behold a white cloud, and upon the cloud one sat like unto the Son of man"

**Text**: "And I looked, and behold a white cloud, and upon the cloud one sat like unto the Son of man, having on his head a golden crown, and in his hand a sharp sickle."

**Exegesis**: The end-time gospel proclamation concludes with a great separation of humanity into two camps, portrayed as dual harvests. Verse 14 opens the harvest section.

**"One sat like unto the Son of man"** (Gr. *homoion huion anthrōpou*): This expression is drawn directly from [Daniel 7:13](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-13.htm) ("one like a son of man" coming on the clouds to receive universal and everlasting dominion from the Ancient of Days). The same phrase is used for Christ in [Rev. 1:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-13.htm). Most likely this is Christ himself rather than an angel as some commentators suggest. The vision also recalls Jesus' own prediction: "You shall see the Son of Man sitting at the right hand of Power, and coming on the clouds of heaven" ([Matt. 26:64](https://biblehub.com/matthew/26-64.htm)); "the sign of the Son of Man coming on the clouds of the sky with power and great glory" ([Matt. 24:30](https://biblehub.com/matthew/24-30.htm)). John announced in the prologue: "He is coming with the clouds, and every eye will see him" ([Rev. 1:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-7.htm)).

**"White cloud"**: The cloud of divine glory and theophany (cf. [Exod. 16:10](https://biblehub.com/exodus/16-10.htm); 40:34–38; [1 Kgs. 8:10](https://biblehub.com/1_kings/8-10.htm)–11; [Matt. 17:5](https://biblehub.com/matthew/17-5.htm); [Acts 1:9](https://biblehub.com/acts/1-9.htm)). Christ comes victoriously on the white cloud to bring judgment.

**"Golden crown"** (Gr. *stephanos*, a crown of victory, not *diadēma*, the royal crown): A garland of triumph — Christ comes as the victor, having completed the gospel proclamation and ready to reap the harvest. The 24 elders also wear *stephanoi* (4:4, 10).

**"Sharp sickle"**: The harvest instrument. The harvest has arrived. The proclamation of the gospel is complete; the sickle is ready.

**Cross-references**: [Dan. 7:13](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-13.htm)–14; [Matt. 24:30](https://biblehub.com/matthew/24-30.htm); 26:64; [Rev. 1:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-7.htm); 1:13; 2:18; 4:4; 14:14.

### [Rev 14:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-15.htm) — "And another angel came out of the temple, crying with a loud voice to him that sat on the cloud"

**Text**: "And another angel came out of the temple, crying with a loud voice to him that sat on the cloud, Thrust in thy sickle, and reap: for the time is come for thee to reap; for the harvest of the earth is ripe."

**Exegesis**: The angel who commands Christ to reap comes from the heavenly *naos* — the most holy place, where God's throne is located (cf. 11:19). This means the angel comes from the very presence of God with a message from God himself. The timing of the harvest is determined by God the Father.

**Command to reap** (Gr. *pempō*, "to send"): "Send forth your sickle." Jesus himself used *pempō* for the eschatological harvest when God "sends the sickle" ([Mark 4:29](https://biblehub.com/mark/4-29.htm)). According to [Matt. 13:39](https://biblehub.com/matthew/13-39.htm)–43, "the reapers are angels" sent by the Son of Man to gather the harvest. The role of angels as reapers is further affirmed in Jesus' Olivet discourse ([Mark 13:27](https://biblehub.com/mark/13-27.htm); [Matt. 24:31](https://biblehub.com/matthew/24-31.htm)). The sending of the sickle in [Rev. 14:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-15.htm) implies the sending of angels to gather the faithful for the kingdom.

**"The harvest of the earth is ripe"** (Gr. *ēxēranthē*, aorist passive of *xērainō*, "to dry out/wither" — suggesting the grain is overripe or fully dried for harvest): The harvest is a biblical metaphor for eschatological judgment on enemies of Israel ([Jer. 51:33](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/51-33.htm); [Joel 3:13](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-13.htm)) and positively for the gathering of God's people ([Hos. 6:11](https://biblehub.com/hosea/6-11.htm); [Joel 3:1](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-1.htm)–2). In Jesus' parables, the harvest marks the end of the age ([Matt. 13:39](https://biblehub.com/matthew/13-39.htm)): "The Son of Man will send forth His angels, and they will gather out of His kingdom all stumbling blocks, and those who commit lawlessness, and they will cast them into the furnace of fire... Then the righteous will shine forth as the sun in the kingdom of their Father" ([Matt. 13:39](https://biblehub.com/matthew/13-39.htm)–43). John the Baptist: Christ "will gather His wheat into the barn, but He will burn up the chaff" ([Matt. 3:12](https://biblehub.com/matthew/3-12.htm)). This first harvest — grain — represents the gathering of the righteous. The proclamation of the gospel has been completed; every person has made their final decision. Probation has closed. The destiny of every human being has been decided.

**Cross-references**: [Jer. 51:33](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/51-33.htm); [Joel 3:13](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-13.htm); [Hos. 6:11](https://biblehub.com/hosea/6-11.htm); [Mark 4:29](https://biblehub.com/mark/4-29.htm); [Matt. 3:12](https://biblehub.com/matthew/3-12.htm); 13:39–43; 24:31; [Rev. 11:19](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-19.htm); 15:5–8.

### [Rev 14:16](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-16.htm) — "And he that sat on the cloud thrust in his sickle on the earth; and the earth was reaped"

**Text**: "And he that sat on the cloud thrust in his sickle on the earth; and the earth was reaped."

**Exegesis**: Christ, the Son of Man on the white cloud, responds immediately to the divine command and swings the sickle. The earth is reaped. The language is crisp and decisive — one stroke, the earth reaped. This is the gathering of the righteous — the 144,000 and the great multitude — prior to the Second Coming. The proclamation of the gospel has divided humanity; the sealing is complete; the grain harvest is the gathering of the redeemed, portrayed as the "firstfruits" (v. 4), into the kingdom ([Matt. 13:43](https://biblehub.com/matthew/13-43.htm); [1 Thess. 4:17](https://biblehub.com/1_thessalonians/4-17.htm)). They are under special protection from the wrath about to be poured out (cf. 7:3; 15:1–6). They are ready for the transformation of their mortal bodies ([1 Cor. 15:50](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/15-50.htm)–54) and to "be caught up together in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air" ([1 Thess. 4:17](https://biblehub.com/1_thessalonians/4-17.htm)). "The harvest is past, summer is ended" ([Jer. 8:20](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/8-20.htm)) — the time of grace has concluded.

**Cross-references**: [Matt. 13:43](https://biblehub.com/matthew/13-43.htm); [Jer. 8:20](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/8-20.htm); [1 Cor. 15:50](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/15-50.htm)–54; [1 Thess. 4:17](https://biblehub.com/1_thessalonians/4-17.htm); [Rev. 7:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-3.htm); 14:4; 15:1–6.

### [Rev 14:17](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-17.htm) — "And another angel came out of the temple which is in heaven, he also having a sharp sickle"

**Text**: "And another angel came out of the temple which is in heaven, he also having a sharp sickle."

**Exegesis**: The grain harvest of God's people is followed by a second and distinct harvesting operation — the vintage harvest of the wicked. A new angel emerges from the heavenly *naos* — also from the very presence of God — bearing his own sharp sickle. The distinction between vv. 14–16 (Son of Man with the sickle for grain) and vv. 17–20 (angel with the sickle for grapes) follows Joel's dual harvest image: "Put in the sickle, for the harvest is ripe. Come, tread, for the wine press is full; the vats overflow, for their wickedness is great" ([Joel 3:13](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-13.htm)). The grain harvest is the gathering of God's people into the kingdom; the grape harvest is the execution of God's wrath on those who sided with the satanic trinity.

**Cross-references**: [Joel 3:13](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-13.htm); [Rev. 14:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-14.htm); 15:5–8.

### [Rev 14:18](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-18.htm) — "And another angel came out from the altar, which had power over fire"

**Text**: "And another angel came out from the altar, which had power over fire; and cried with a loud cry to him that had the sharp sickle, saying, Thrust in thy sharp sickle, and gather the clusters of the vine of the earth; for her grapes are fully ripe."

**Exegesis**: A second angel in this sequence (the fourth overall in ch. 14) comes specifically from the *altar* — most likely the altar of burnt offering referenced in the fifth seal (6:9) and in 8:3–5, rather than the golden altar of incense. Beneath this altar in the fifth seal, the martyred saints have been crying for judgment: "How long, O Lord, holy and true, will you not judge and avenge our blood upon those who dwell on the earth?" (6:10). They were told to wait until the appointed time.

**"Having authority over fire"**: This identifies him with the angel of [Rev. 8:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-5.htm) who fills the censer with fire from the altar and throws it to the earth, followed by thunder, lightning, and earthquake as the announcement of approaching divine judgment. Fire from the altar carries the prayers of the saints to God's throne and signals the commencement of divine judgment.

**Connection**: The altar angel's appearance now indicates that the prayers of the martyred saints are finally being answered. The gospel has been proclaimed; God's final warning has been given; the sins of the oppressors have reached their full measure; God's people are sealed and complete. The time has come for God to fulfill his promise and deal with the opponents of the gospel and the oppressors of his faithful people.

**"Thrust in thy sharp sickle"** (Gr. *pempō*, "to send"): The same verb as in v. 15, drawing [Joel 3:13](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-13.htm) into direct parallel. The grapes of the earth are "fully ripe" (Gr. *ēkmasen*, "reached their peak ripeness"). The wickedness of those who refused the gospel is now at its full measure.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 6:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/6-9.htm)–11; 8:3–5; [Joel 3:13](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-13.htm); [Rev. 14:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-15.htm).

### [Rev 14:19](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-19.htm) — "And the angel thrust in his sickle into the earth, and gathered the vine of the earth"

**Text**: "And the angel thrust in his sickle into the earth, and gathered the vine of the earth, and cast it into the great winepress of the wrath of God."

**Exegesis**: The angel swings his sickle, gathers the vintage of the earth, and casts it into the great winepress of the wrath of God. The imagery of treading grapes in the winepress was well-known throughout the ancient Near East.

**The winepress** (as described by Barclay): An upper and a lower trough connected by a channel, either hollowed in rock or built of brick. Grapes are placed in the upper trough, trampled by foot, and the juice flows into the lower trough.

**OT background — [Isaiah 63:1](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/63-1.htm)–6**: The primary background text. God himself is portrayed as the divine warrior returning after executing his wrath upon Edom: "I have trodden the wine trough alone, and from the peoples there was no man with Me. I also trod them in My anger, and trampled them in My wrath; and their lifeblood is sprinkled on My garments, and I stained all My raiment." [Lamentations 1:15](https://biblehub.com/lamentations/1-15.htm) adds: "The Lord has trodden as in a winepress the virgin daughter of Judah." John employs this well-known imagery to portray the execution of God's wrath in the closing days. [Revelation 19:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/19-13.htm)–15 describes Christ the Warrior treading "the winepress of the wine of the anger of the wrath of God, the Almighty."

**Symbolic meaning**: The casting of the grapes into the winepress and their trampling is the symbolic portrayal of the seven last plagues (chs. 15–16), in which "the wrath of God was completed" (15:1).

**"Outside the city"** (v. 20): Joel's prophecy specifically situates the judgment in the valley of Jehoshaphat which lay outside the walls of Jerusalem — "Put in the sickle, for the harvest is ripe. Come, tread, for the wine press is full; the vats overflow, for their wickedness is great" ([Joel 3:13](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-13.htm); cf. [Zech. 14:2](https://biblehub.com/zechariah/14-2.htm)–4). This city is the New Jerusalem, "into which nothing unclean can enter" (21:27). The judgment of the wicked occurs outside the holy city.

**Cross-references**: [Isa. 63:1](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/63-1.htm)–6; [Lam. 1:15](https://biblehub.com/lamentations/1-15.htm); [Joel 3:2](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-2.htm)–3, 13; [Zech. 14:2](https://biblehub.com/zechariah/14-2.htm)–4; [Rev. 15:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/15-1.htm); 19:13–15; 21:27.

### [Rev 14:20](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-20.htm) — "And the winepress was trodden without the city, and blood came out of the winepress"

**Text**: "And the winepress was trodden without the city, and blood came out of the winepress, even unto the horse bridles, by the space of a thousand and six hundred furlongs."

**Exegesis**: The vision concludes with hyperbolic imagery of devastating scope.

**"Without the city"**: As noted above, this fulfills [Joel 3:2](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-2.htm)–3, 13 — the judgment of the nations in the valley of Jehoshaphat outside Jerusalem. John reinterprets the originally ethnic and local prophecy, giving it worldwide eschatological significance.

**"Blood came out of the winepress, even unto the horse bridles"**: This is a hyperbolic expression from battle imagery well-known in Jewish literature. 1 Enoch (a Jewish pseudepigraphal book) provides a direct parallel: "The horse shall walk through the blood of sinners up to his chest; and the chariot shall sink down up to its top. In those days, the angels shall descend into the secret places... and the Most High will arise on that day of judgment in order to execute a great judgment upon all the sinners." This confirms that the hyperbole was a conventional Jewish idiom for describing the severity of the final divine judgment — not a literal prediction of geography or quantity.

**"A thousand and six hundred furlongs"** (1,600 *stadia*, approximately 184 miles or 300 km): No fully satisfying interpretation has been established. Three main views:

1. *Geographical*: 1,600 stadia corresponds roughly to the length of Palestine from north to south. Palestine thus stands for the entire earth as the battlefield completely covered with blood.

2. *Symbolic — four squared times one hundred*: Four is the number of the earth (four corners, [Rev. 7:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-1.htm); 20:8; four winds; four quarters, [Isa. 11:12](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/11-12.htm)). 4 × 4 × 100 = 1,600, symbolizing the worldwide scope of the execution of divine wrath.

3. *Rhetorical*: The SDA Bible Commentary provides the most likely intent: "The main thought is that the enemies of the church of God are to be completely and finally overthrown. Therefore, the church can look forward to full and complete deliverance from all her enemies, and to joyful triumph in the kingdom of God." The grotesque imagery is not cartographic but is intended to underscore the totality and finality of divine judgment.

**Purpose of the entire harvest section (vv. 14–20)**: Together with the three angels' messages, the dual harvest "warn the readers of the seriousness of their decision to respond to the gospel warning proclaimed by the three angels. The only way to escape the fate reserved for the satanic triumvirate and their worshipers is to choose to serve the true God and worship him" (Stefanovic). Fear of the beast's threats is driven out by "a stronger fear" — the same method Jesus used: "fear Him who is able to destroy both soul and body in hell" ([Matt. 10:28](https://biblehub.com/matthew/10-28.htm)).

**Cross-references**: [Joel 3:2](https://biblehub.com/joel/3-2.htm)–3, 13; [Zech. 14:2](https://biblehub.com/zechariah/14-2.htm)–4; 1 Enoch 100:3; [Isa. 11:12](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/11-12.htm); [Rev. 7:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-1.htm); 15:1; 19:13–21; 20:8; 21:27; [Matt. 10:28](https://biblehub.com/matthew/10-28.htm).

**Chapter Summary** (Stefanovic/structural): Revelation 14 occupies the structural position of segment E in the chiastic outline of chs. 12–22 (E' parallel to E, i.e., the church proclaims the end-time gospel, 14:1–20, parallel to Rev. 10–11:18). The chapter divides into: (a) the victorious 144,000 on Mount Zion (vv. 1–5) — the eschatological outcome presented before the gospel call; (b) the three angels' messages (vv. 6–12) — God's final warning to the world; (c) the second beatitude (v. 13) — assurance to those who die faithful; (d) the dual harvest (vv. 14–20) — the grain harvest of the righteous (vv. 14–16) and the grape harvest/winepress of the wicked (vv. 17–20), portraying the great eschatological separation immediately preceding the Second Coming and the pouring out of the seven last plagues (chs. 15–16). The three angels' messages are the antithesis of the three demonic spirits of 16:13–14; the seal of God (14:1) is the antithesis of the mark of the beast (13:15–16); the woman-city of New Jerusalem/Zion is the antithesis of Babylon (chs. 17–18).
