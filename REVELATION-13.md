## Revelation 13

**Structural Overview**: Chapter 13 presents the dragon's two allies in the eschatological war against God's people (12:17). The sea beast (vv. 1–10) is introduced in general description drawn from Daniel 7 (vv. 1–4), then identified by his activities during forty-two months (vv. 5–7), with v. 8 serving as the hinge toward the final conflict (vv. 11–18). The earth beast (vv. 11–18) mirrors the sea beast as the Holy Spirit mirrors Christ in a satanic parody of the Trinity. Together, the dragon + sea beast + earth beast form the satanic triumvirate opposed to Father + Christ + Holy Spirit.

---

### [Rev 13:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-1.htm) — "And I stood upon the sand of the sea"

**Text**: "And I stood upon the sand of the sea, and saw a beast rise up out of the sea, having seven heads and ten horns, and upon his horns ten crowns, and upon his heads the name of blasphemy."

**Exegesis**: The KJV reading "I stood" (Gk. *estathen*) is based on later Greek manuscripts; the preferred early manuscript reading is "he stood" (*estathe*), meaning the **dragon** stands on the seashore to summon his first ally. This is the more accurate reading adopted by modern translations. The dragon stations himself at the boundary between sea and land to call forth the beast from the waters.

**The beast** (Gk. *therion*): a wild, savage beast — a being of bestial, monstrous nature. The term denotes not merely an animal but a creature of destructive, demonic character. In Revelation, *therion* consistently symbolizes the political power through which Satan operates throughout earth's history (11:7; 13:1–18; 14:9–11; 15:2; 16:2, 10, 13; 17:3–17; 19:19–20; 20:4, 10).

**The sea** as origin is a direct allusion to [Daniel 7:2](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-2.htm)–3, where the four world empires rise from the great sea. In the OT, the sea symbolizes the abode of sea monsters ([Job 26:12](https://biblehub.com/job/26-12.htm)–13; [Ps. 74:13](https://biblehub.com/psalms/74-13.htm)–14; [Isa. 27:1](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/27-1.htm); 51:9–10; [Ezek. 32:2](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/32-2.htm)), the source of evil powers that oppressed Israel. Corsini argues this sea "represents the cosmic yet historical reality of the bottomless pit." [Revelation 17:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-8.htm) identifies this same beast as coming "out of the abyss," confirming that the sea and the abyss are equivalent symbols. [Revelation 17:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-15.htm) further equates the "many waters" with "peoples and multitudes and nations and tongues," so the sea symbolizes "the disturbed and stormy social and political conditions out of which tyrannies commonly arise."

**Seven heads and ten horns**: The seven heads represent "the kingdoms through which Satan has worked to oppress God's people throughout the ages" (Johnsson). The beast was active in one head after another; when one head was mortally wounded, activity ceased; when healed, it resumes. The ten horns correspond to [Daniel 7:7](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-7.htm), 23–24, symbolizing kingdoms arising after the breakup of Rome (cf. [Rev. 17:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-12.htm)). Crucially, the Daniel 7 beasts together have seven heads and ten horns in total — the sea beast **incorporates all four Danielic empires** into a single composite entity.

**Ten crowns** on the horns are *diadems* (*diadema*) — royal crowns of sovereignty — given by the dragon (v. 2), representing secular and political authority delegated by Satan (cf. [Rev. 17:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-12.htm)–13, 17). This contrasts with Christ who wears "many crowns" (*diadema*) as "Lord of lords and King of kings" ([Rev. 17:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-14.htm); 19:12). The sea beast mirrors the dragon of 12:3 (same heads and horns), implying a oneness of dragon and sea beast that parodies the oneness of Father and Son ([John 14:9](https://biblehub.com/john/14-9.htm)).

**Names of blasphemy** on the heads: blasphemy in the NT is the act of claiming equality with God ([John 10:33](https://biblehub.com/john/10-33.htm); cf. [Matt. 26:63](https://biblehub.com/matthew/26-63.htm)–65) and claiming divine prerogatives ([Mark 2:7](https://biblehub.com/mark/2-7.htm)). The blasphemous names indicate this power arrogates to itself what belongs to God alone — self-deification, demanding worship that belongs only to the Creator (cf. [2 Thess. 2:3](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-3.htm)–4).

**Cross-references**: [Dan. 7:2](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-2.htm)–7, 17, 23–24; [Ps. 74:13](https://biblehub.com/psalms/74-13.htm)–14; [Job 26:12](https://biblehub.com/job/26-12.htm)–13; [Isa. 27:1](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/27-1.htm); [Rev. 11:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-7.htm); 12:3; 17:3–15; [2 Thess. 2:3](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-3.htm)–4.

### [Rev 13:2](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-2.htm) — "The beast which I saw was like unto a leopard"

**Text**: "And the beast which I saw was like unto a leopard, and his feet were as the feet of a bear, and his mouth as the mouth of a lion: and the dragon gave him his power, and his seat, and great authority."

**Exegesis**: The composite beast reverses the order of Daniel 7's four beasts (lion → bear → leopard → ten-horned beast), recapitulating the entire succession of world empires from Babylon to Rome in a single entity. The leopard body ([Dan. 7:6](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-6.htm) = Greece/Alexander), bear's feet ([Dan. 7:5](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-5.htm) = Medo-Persia), and lion's mouth ([Dan. 7:4](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-4.htm) = Babylon) together portray the sea beast as the **real successor** of all preceding powers, inheriting their combined ferocity, speed, and crushing strength. Daniel 7 thus provides the hermeneutical key for identifying the sea beast — since in Daniel a beast stands for a ruling power, the sea beast is a ruling power, both political and religious, that emerges after Rome's breakup.

**The dragon gave him his power, and his seat (throne), and great authority**: This investiture is the satanic parallel to the Father conferring authority on Christ ([Rev. 2:27](https://biblehub.com/revelation/2-27.htm); [Matt. 28:18](https://biblehub.com/matthew/28-18.htm)). Satan claimed dominion over "all the kingdoms of the world" ([Luke 4:6](https://biblehub.com/luke/4-6.htm): "it has been handed over to me, and I give it to whomever I wish"). This claim is now operationalized through the sea beast via the Roman Empire. The beast's "throne" (*thronos*) stands in opposition to "the throne of God" (cf. [Rev. 2:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/2-13.htm); 16:10). The triumvirate (dragon + sea beast + earth beast) is thus a satanic parody of the Trinity, with the sea beast as the anti-Christ figure receiving authority from the anti-Father.

**Cross-references**: [Dan. 7:3](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-3.htm)–7; [Luke 4:6](https://biblehub.com/luke/4-6.htm); [John 12:31](https://biblehub.com/john/12-31.htm); 14:30; [Rev. 2:27](https://biblehub.com/revelation/2-27.htm); 12:3.

### [Rev 13:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-3.htm) — "One of his heads as it were wounded to death"

**Text**: "And I saw one of his heads as it were wounded to death; and his deadly wound was healed: and all the world wondered after the beast."

**Exegesis**: **"Slain"** (Gk. *sphazō*): the same verb used for the slaughter of the Lamb in [Rev. 5:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/5-6.htm), 9, 12 and 13:8. The deliberate use of identical vocabulary — a beast-head "slain" like the Lamb — establishes a parody of Christ's death. The mortal wound thus mimics the crucifixion. The same word is also used for the death of God's faithful martyrs (6:9; 18:24) and in [1 [John 3:12](https://biblehub.com/john/3-12.htm)](https://biblehub.com/1_john/3-12.htm) for Cain slaying Abel.

**Which head is wounded?** Most likely the seventh, which according to [Rev. 17:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-9.htm)–10 "was not" yet from John's perspective but "is to come." The beast had existed through history in successive heads; when one was mortally wounded, the beast's dominion temporarily ceased. The text of [Rev. 13:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-14.htm) clarifies that the wound was caused "by the sword" — a politically inflicted blow.

**The mortal wound healed**: This is the spurious resurrection, the anti-resurrection paralleling Christ's rising from the dead. The beast "was, and is not, and is about to come up out of the abyss" ([Rev. 17:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-8.htm)) mirrors the Christological formula of one "who was and who is and who is to come" ([Rev. 1:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-4.htm), 8). The healing triggers worldwide admiration (*ethaumasthē hē holē gē*) and ultimately worship (v. 4). Historically, Stefanovic identifies this mortal wound with the demise of the medieval ecclesiastical-political order — the events surrounding the French Revolution and the neutralization of papal temporal power under Napoleon — following the long process of Renaissance skepticism, Protestant challenge, Enlightenment secularization, and the emergence of republican governance. The wound ushers the beast into the "is not" phase ([Rev. 17:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-11.htm)), after which healing inaugurates the beast's final phase of activity (the eighth head, [Rev. 17:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-11.htm)).

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 5:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/5-6.htm), 9, 12; 6:9; 13:8, 12, 14; 17:8–11; 18:24; [1 [John 3:12](https://biblehub.com/john/3-12.htm)](https://biblehub.com/1_john/3-12.htm).

### [Rev 13:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-4.htm) — "They worshipped the dragon which gave power unto the beast"

**Text**: "And they worshipped the dragon which gave power unto the beast: and they worshipped the beast, saying, Who is like unto the beast? who is able to make war with him?"

**Exegesis**: The healing of the mortal wound produces two objects of worship: the dragon and the beast. This constitutes the eschatological inversion of true worship ([Rev. 14:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-7.htm) — "Fear God and give him glory ... worship him who made"). The rhetoric moves from admiration (v. 3) to outright worship, fusing political awe with religious devotion. The beast thus functions as simultaneously political and religious power, as Stefanovic emphasizes.

**"Who is like unto the beast?"** (*tis homoios tō thēriō*): a direct parody of the OT doxological question "Who is like God?" — *mi kamokha* — which occurs in [Exod. 15:11](https://biblehub.com/exodus/15-11.htm) ("Who is like you, O LORD, among the gods?"), [Ps. 35:10](https://biblehub.com/psalms/35-10.htm), and [Mic. 7:18](https://biblehub.com/micah/7-18.htm). This is especially significant because Christ, while fighting the dragon in heaven (12:7), is called **Michael** — Hebrew *mika'el*, meaning "Who is like God?" The beast's worshipers echo this divine name in honor of the beast, inverting the confession that belongs to God alone.

**"Who is able to make war with him?"**: The implied answer is "no one," expressing absolute confidence in the beast's invincibility — a direct counterfeit of God's omnipotence. This prepares the ground for the war against the saints (v. 7).

**Cross-references**: [Exod. 15:11](https://biblehub.com/exodus/15-11.htm); [Ps. 35:10](https://biblehub.com/psalms/35-10.htm); [Mic. 7:18](https://biblehub.com/micah/7-18.htm); [Rev. 12:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-7.htm); 14:7; 19:10.

### [Rev 13:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-5.htm) — "A mouth speaking great things and blasphemies"

**Text**: "And there was given unto him a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies; and power was given unto him to continue forty and two months."

**Exegesis**: The passive constructions ("was given") throughout vv. 5–7 indicate divine permission — God permits but does not ordain this activity. This alludes directly to [Daniel 7:8](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-8.htm) and 25, where the symbolic little horn arising after the fourth beast (Rome) speaks "great things against the Most High" and "wears out the saints." The sea beast is thus identified with Daniel's little horn.

**Forty-two months** = 3.5 years = 1,260 days ([Rev. 11:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-3.htm); 12:6) = "a time, times, and half a time" ([Dan. 7:25](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-25.htm); 12:7; [Rev. 12:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-14.htm)). All these expressions are equivalent, drawn from Daniel's prophetic framework. This is the period during which the nations trample the holy city ([Rev. 11:2](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-2.htm)) and the dragon pursues the woman in the wilderness ([Rev. 12:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-6.htm), 13–16). Stefanovic emphasizes that this symbolic period corresponds to approximately 1,200 years of medieval religious-political oppression of God's people. Theologically, the forty-two months also parallels Christ's three-and-a-half year earthly ministry — a period characterized by continual rejection and persecution: "If they persecuted me, they will also persecute you" ([John 15:20](https://biblehub.com/john/15-20.htm)).

**"Speaking great things"** (*megala*): claims to divine authority, arrogating to itself prerogatives belonging only to God.

**Cross-references**: [Dan. 7:8](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-8.htm), 25; [Rev. 11:2](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-2.htm)–3; 12:6, 14; 13:1–2.

### [Rev 13:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-6.htm) — "He opened his mouth in blasphemy against God"

**Text**: "And he opened his mouth in blasphemy against God, to blaspheme his name, and his tabernacle, and them that dwell in heaven."

**Exegesis**: Three objects of blasphemy: (1) **God's name** — his character, titles, and attributes; (2) **his tabernacle** (*skēnē*) — the heavenly sanctuary, the locus of God's presence and Christ's intercessory ministry; (3) **"them that dwell in heaven"** — God's faithful people.

The identification of God's tabernacle with "those who dwell in heaven" is theologically rich: God's dwelling-place is equated with his covenant people who, though physically on earth, are spiritually "seated with him in the heavenly places, in Christ Jesus" ([Eph. 2:6](https://biblehub.com/ephesians/2-6.htm)). Revelation consistently distinguishes two groups: the wicked as "those who dwell on the earth" (vv. 8, 12, 14; cf. 6:10; 8:13; 11:10) versus the righteous as "those who dwell in heaven" — i.e., those in spiritual union with Christ (cf. [Rev. 1:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-6.htm); 5:9–10). Blasphemy against the tabernacle is thus an attack on both Christ's heavenly priestly ministry and on his people below.

Ladd defines the beast's blasphemies as "the derogation of deity by his own claim to self-deification" — not merely cursing God but arrogating divine status and prerogatives to itself (cf. [2 Thess. 2:4](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-4.htm): "He takes his seat in the temple of God, displaying himself as being God").

**Cross-references**: [John 10:33](https://biblehub.com/john/10-33.htm); [Eph. 2:6](https://biblehub.com/ephesians/2-6.htm); [2 Thess. 2:4](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-4.htm); [Rev. 6:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/6-10.htm); 11:10; 13:8, 12, 14.

### [Rev 13:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-7.htm) — "Power was given him over all kindreds, and tongues, and nations"

**Text**: "And it was given unto him to make war with the saints, and to overcome them: and power was given him over all kindreds, and tongues, and nations."

**Exegesis**: Again the passive (*edothē autō*) — divine permission. The beast is given authority to war against and **overcome** (*nikēsai*) the saints — temporary military-political victory over God's people. This alludes to [Daniel 7:21](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-21.htm), 25 (the little horn "was waging war with the saints and overpowering them"). The saints do not escape suffering; persecution is their lot during the forty-two months. Yet they overcome ultimately through the blood of the Lamb and the word of their testimony ([Rev. 12:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-11.htm)).

**"Every tribe and people and tongue and nation"** (*pan phylēn kai laon kai glōssan kai ethnos*): the fourfold formula denotes the universal territorial scope of the beast's authority. Critically, this is the identical formula used in [Rev. 14:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-6.htm) for the scope of the end-time gospel proclamation ("to every nation and tribe and tongue and people"). The sea beast thus **offers a rival gospel** to the three angels of Revelation 14, competing for the same global audience. His is a counter-missionary movement.

The giving of this authority echoes [Rev. 13:2](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-2.htm) (the dragon's delegation). As Christ received universal authority from the Father ([Matt. 28:18](https://biblehub.com/matthew/28-18.htm)), so the beast receives it from the dragon. The beast and Christ exercise parallel global authority — underscoring the antichrist character of this power.

**Cross-references**: [Dan. 7:21](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-21.htm), 25; [Matt. 28:18](https://biblehub.com/matthew/28-18.htm); [Rev. 12:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-11.htm); 14:6; 17:15.

### [Rev 13:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-8.htm) — "All that dwell upon the earth shall worship him"

**Text**: "And all that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not written in the book of life of the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world."

**Exegesis**: Verse 8 functions as the hinge of Chapter 13: it concludes the description of the sea beast's activities and introduces the final conflict. This is an eschatological statement projecting to the time of the end — the same power that oppressed God's people throughout the medieval period will again dominate and demand the allegiance of the entire world.

**Two groups in the final crisis are definitively demarcated**: (1) those who worship the beast — "those who dwell on the earth" — whose names are **not** in the book of life; (2) those who worship God — whose names **are** written in the book of life. The book of life is "the book of life of the **Lamb**" — Christocentric: the only basis of enrollment is the redemptive work of Christ. This sets up the great warning of [Rev. 14:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-6.htm)–12 (three angels' messages): fear God and give him glory, do not worship the beast.

**"The Lamb slain from the foundation of the world"** (*tou arniou tou esphagmenou apo katabolēs kosmou*): the participial phrase is deliberately ambiguous — it can modify "the Lamb" (slain before the foundation) or "names written" (enrolled from the foundation). The Greek syntax supports taking it with "the Lamb" — Christ's sacrifice was purposed and effective from creation's beginning (cf. [1 Pet. 1:19](https://biblehub.com/1_peter/1-19.htm)–20; [Heb. 9:26](https://biblehub.com/hebrews/9-26.htm)). Stefanovic: "the only hope of God's people in the final conflict is in the blood of Christ and the salvation that he secured by his death on the cross."

**The book of life** (*biblion tēs zōēs*): cf. [Rev. 3:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/3-5.htm); 17:8; 20:12, 15; 21:27. Names blotted from it indicate apostasy; names retained indicate perseverance. The Lamb's book of life contrasts with the beast's mark — the two ultimate signs of allegiance.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 3:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/3-5.htm); 14:6–12; 17:8; 20:12, 15; 21:27; [1 Pet. 1:19](https://biblehub.com/1_peter/1-19.htm)–20; [Heb. 9:26](https://biblehub.com/hebrews/9-26.htm).

### [Rev 13:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-9.htm) — "If any man have an ear, let him hear"

**Text**: "If any man have an ear, let him hear."

**Exegesis**: This appeal formula is identical to the refrain that closes each of Christ's seven letters to the churches in Revelation 2–3 (2:7, 11, 17, 29; 3:6, 13, 22; cf. also 13:9). Its repetition here without "what the Spirit says to the churches" is notable — it universalizes the call, urging all humanity (not only the covenant community) to heed what follows. The solemn call prepares the reader for the double proverb in v. 10.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 2:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/2-7.htm), 11, 17, 29; 3:6, 13, 22; [Matt. 11:15](https://biblehub.com/matthew/11-15.htm); 13:9, 43.

### [Rev 13:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-10.htm) — "He that leadeth into captivity shall go into captivity"

**Text**: "He that leadeth into captivity shall go into captivity: he that killeth with the sword must be killed with the sword. Here is the patience and the faith of the saints."

**Exegesis**: The verse comprises a double proverb followed by a theological affirmation.

**Textual note**: Two readings exist. (1) KJV/RSV/NASB reading (supported by Codex Sinaiticus, 4th century): "if somebody kills with the sword, with the sword he must be killed" — emphasizing the *persecutor's* destiny. (2) NIV/NEB reading (Codex Alexandrinus, 5th century): "if anyone is to be killed with the sword, with the sword he will be killed" — emphasizing the *saints'* destiny. Stefanovic favors the Sinaiticus reading, since it echoes [Jeremiah 15:2](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/15-2.htm) (judgment on apostate Israel) and [Jeremiah 43:11](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/43-11.htm) (judgment on Egypt), and Jesus' word in [Matt. 26:52](https://biblehub.com/matthew/26-52.htm) ("all who take up the sword shall perish by the sword").

**First statement** (captivity): describes the destiny of God's faithful — their loyalty will result in imprisonment (cf. [Rev. 12:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-11.htm)). They are not promised escape.

**Second statement** (sword): describes the destiny of the persecutors — God's people are not to take up the sword in self-defense. God himself will bring proportionate retribution (cf. [Rev. 18:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/18-6.htm)–8: "Pay her back as she has paid, and give back to her double").

**"Here is the patience and the faith of the saints"** (*hē hypomonē kai hē pistis tōn hagiōn*): the saints' defining virtues during the end-time crisis. *Hypomonē* = patient endurance, steadfast perseverance under pressure. *Pistis* = faith, faithfulness, fidelity. While the satanic triumvirate seeks universal allegiance from "those who dwell on the earth," God's people are characterized by unswerving faithfulness with patient endurance (cf. [Rev. 14:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-12.htm): "Here is the endurance of the saints who keep the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus"). God's judgment is the last word; his people do not need to seize it.

**Cross-references**: [Jer. 15:2](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/15-2.htm); 43:11; [Matt. 26:52](https://biblehub.com/matthew/26-52.htm); [Rev. 12:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-11.htm); 14:12; 18:6–8.

### [Rev 13:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-11.htm) — "Another beast coming up out of the earth"

**Text**: "And I beheld another beast coming up out of the earth; and he had two horns like a lamb, and he spake as a dragon."

**Exegesis**: Satan's second ally is introduced with the same term *therion* (savage beast) as the sea beast, but his description is radically different. Where the sea beast is monstrous, this beast appears lamb-like.

**"Out of the earth"** (Gk. *gē*): the same earth that in [Rev. 12:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-15.htm)–16 helped the woman by swallowing the dragon's floodlike river — a positive symbol. The earth beast thus begins as a friendly, benevolent power. In the time of the end, however, this same earth "becomes the place of abominations of eschatological Babylon" ([Rev. 17:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-5.htm); 19:2). The association of sea and earth (Rev. 13) mirrors Rev. 10 and fulfills the proclamation of [Rev. 12:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-12.htm): "Woe to the earth and the sea, because the devil has come down to you." Together they signify the universal scope of Satan's end-time activity.

**"Two horns like a lamb"**: in Revelation, the lamb (*arnion*) always refers to Christ (5:6; 7:17; 14:1, etc.). The deliberate echo indicates this beast appears **Christlike** — tolerant, gentle, bearing religious authority with two horns (possibly symbolic of church and state functioning in apparent harmony). Schüssler Fiorenza: "the reference to the horns and the speech of the monster further affirms the assertion that the text does not refer to any lamb and dragon, but clearly to the Lamb and the Dragon." This is therefore the arch-deceiver of the final crisis, whose power lies precisely in appearing to be what he is not.

**"He spake as a dragon"**: his character — speech, policy, legislation — betrays the dragon's character. This may evoke the serpent's "seductive and deceitful" speech in the Garden (Gen. 3; [Rev. 12:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-9.htm)). As OT prophets were God's mouthpiece, this end-time power is Satan's mouthpiece. This is why the rest of Revelation calls the earth beast **"the false prophet"** ([Rev. 16:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-13.htm); 19:20; 20:10).

Stefanovic identifies the earth beast as an exclusively end-time power with no ancient antecedents — arising after the sea beast receives its mortal wound, during the beast's "is not" phase ([Rev. 17:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-11.htm)). He notes that the United States — emerging after the medieval church's demise, characterized by religious and political tolerance (lamb-like), yet capable of exercising worldwide political and military authority — most closely matches this description in modern history.

**Cross-references**: [Gen. 3:1](https://biblehub.com/genesis/3-1.htm); [Rev. 12:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-9.htm), 15–16; 16:13; 19:20; 20:10.

### [Rev 13:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-12.htm) — "He exerciseth all the power of the first beast before him"

**Text**: "And he exerciseth all the power of the first beast before him, and causeth the earth and them which dwell therein to worship the first beast, whose deadly wound was healed."

**Exegesis**: The earth beast is the satanic parody of the **Holy Spirit**. As the Spirit exercises the authority of Christ ([John 15:26](https://biblehub.com/john/15-26.htm); 16:13–14), magnifying and glorifying Christ, so the earth beast exercises the authority of the sea beast, exalting and directing worship toward him. This is the clearest structural indication of the satanic Trinity: dragon (anti-Father) → sea beast (anti-Christ) → earth beast (anti-Holy Spirit / false prophet).

**"Before him"** (*enōpion autou*): "in his presence," in the service of the sea beast. The earth beast acts as a subordinate agent in the sea beast's campaign for universal worship.

**"Causeth the earth and them which dwell therein to worship"**: The Greek *poiei* ("makes/causes") is present tense, while *proskynesousin* ("to worship") is future — indicating the earth beast is presently engaged in preparing the world for future worship of the first beast. This "making" involves, in its initial stage, deception and persuasion (vv. 13–14), and ultimately coercion (vv. 15–17).

**The mortal wound** is referenced again (cf. v. 3) — the healing is the catalyst for the beast's eschatological resurgence and the earth beast's campaign on his behalf. The healed wound generates the worldwide awe (v. 3) that the earth beast exploits to redirect into worship.

**Cross-references**: [John 15:26](https://biblehub.com/john/15-26.htm); 16:12–15; [Rev. 13:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-3.htm)–4; 16:13.

### [Rev 13:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-13.htm) — "He doeth great wonders, so that he maketh fire come down from heaven"

**Text**: "And he doeth great wonders, so that he maketh fire come down from heaven on the earth in the sight of men,"

**Exegesis**: The earth beast's first strategy is **counterfeit miraculous signs** (*sēmeia megala*, "great signs"). Signs (*sēmeia*) is the language of apostolic authentication: "Miracles and wonders and signs" characterized Jesus' earthly ministry ([Acts 2:22](https://biblehub.com/acts/2-22.htm)), and the Holy Spirit worked signs and wonders through the apostles ([Acts 2:43](https://biblehub.com/acts/2-43.htm); 4:30; 5:12–16). The false prophet replicates this pattern to claim divine authorization.

**"Fire come down from heaven"**: This carries two primary allusions:
1. **Elijah at Mount Carmel** ([1 Kings 18:38](https://biblehub.com/1_kings/18-38.htm)) — calling fire from heaven to demonstrate that Yahweh alone is God. The earth beast performs the same sign but in service of a false god, functioning as a **counterfeit Elijah** — a sign that God, not the beast, is the true sovereign, but which is twisted to promote the beast's worship.
2. **Pentecost** ([Acts 2:3](https://biblehub.com/acts/2-3.htm)) — the tongues of fire descending from heaven inaugurated the apostolic age and authenticated the gospel. The earth beast's fire is a counterfeit Pentecost, authenticating the beast's counter-gospel.

The "great signs" performed "in the sight of men" (*enōpion tōn anthrōpōn*) — publicly, visibly — are designed to be maximally compelling. Paul anticipated this in [2 Thess. 2:8](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-8.htm)–10: the lawless one comes "in accord with the activity of Satan, with all power and signs and false wonders, and with all the deception of wickedness for those who perish."

**Cross-references**: [1 Kings 18:38](https://biblehub.com/1_kings/18-38.htm); [Acts 2:3](https://biblehub.com/acts/2-3.htm), 22, 43; [2 Thess. 2:8](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-8.htm)–10; [Rev. 16:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-13.htm)–14; 19:20.

### [Rev 13:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-14.htm) — "And deceiveth them that dwell on the earth by the means of those miracles"

**Text**: "And deceiveth them that dwell on the earth by the means of those miracles which he had power to do in the sight of the beast; saying to them that dwell on the earth, that they should make an image to the beast, which had the wound by a sword, and did live."

**Exegesis**: **Deception** (*planā*): the earth beast's miracles are explicitly called deceptive — their purpose is not to reveal truth but to mislead. The authority for these signs is Satanically derived ("given to him" — cf. v. 13), and he performs them "in the sight of the beast," i.e., in the beast's presence and service.

**"Make an image to the beast"** (*poiēsai eikona tō thēriō*): a new actor enters the eschatological drama — **the image of the beast**. This image is distinct from both the sea beast and the earth beast. It is constructed at the direction of the earth beast and has a primarily political function (coercive force), while the earth beast operates through persuasion. Desmond Ford: the image points to "an institution and procedures which will duplicate the form and behavior of the beast power in other ages. That union of church and state which characterizes apostasy, and ever precedes persecution, is again to be made." The final conflict will be "the ultimate union of professed Christianity with the power of the state, in order to force all men to conform to its decrees."

The **primary OT archetype** is **Daniel 3** — Nebuchadnezzar's golden image, 60 cubits high and 6 cubits wide (itself bearing numerical significance to Babylonian sexagesimal religion), with the death penalty for all who refused to worship. As in Daniel's day worship of an image was enforced by legislative decree, so at the end the demand to worship the image of the beast will be supported by civil power.

The wound "by a sword" (*en tē machaira*) and resurrection are reiterated, emphasizing that the beast's claim to messianic authority rests on his spurious death-and-resurrection narrative.

**Cross-references**: [Dan. 3:1](https://biblehub.com/daniel/3-1.htm)–18; [2 Thess. 2:8](https://biblehub.com/2_thessalonians/2-8.htm)–10; [Rev. 16:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-13.htm)–14.

### [Rev 13:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-15.htm) — "He had power to give life unto the image of the beast"

**Text**: "And he had power to give life unto the image of the beast, that the image of the beast should both speak, and cause that as many as would not worship the image of the beast should be killed."

**Exegesis**: The earth beast **animates** the image — gives it breath (*pneuma*) so that it speaks and acts. This parodies the creation of humanity ([Gen. 2:7](https://biblehub.com/genesis/2-7.htm), where God breathes life into the dust of the ground). OT polemics against idols repeatedly mock their inability to speak or act (Pss. 115:4–5; 135:15, 17; [Isa. 46:7](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/46-7.htm); [Jer. 10:5](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/10-5.htm), 14; [Hab. 2:18](https://biblehub.com/habakkuk/2-18.htm)–19) — they have no breath. By giving the image breath, the earth beast claims the creative prerogative of the living God.

The **image speaks** (*lalēsē*): this animation gives the image legislative and judicial authority. Its first act is to **decree death** for all who refuse worship — a direct parallel to [Daniel 3:6](https://biblehub.com/daniel/3-6.htm) ("whoever does not fall down and worship shall immediately be cast into the midst of a furnace of blazing fire"). This marks the transition from persuasion to **coercion** in the satanic strategy. Deception (vv. 13–14) fails to capture all; compulsion (vv. 15–17) is brought to bear on the remainder.

**The issue in the final crisis is worship** ([Rev. 14:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-7.htm); 19:10; 22:8–9 — only God is worthy of worship). The two sides: those who worship God vs. those who worship the dragon and the beast ([Rev. 14:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-9.htm)–11; 16:2; 19:20; 20:4). The death decree has no weapon against those who love not their lives unto death ([Rev. 12:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-11.htm)).

**Cross-references**: [Gen. 2:7](https://biblehub.com/genesis/2-7.htm); [Dan. 3:6](https://biblehub.com/daniel/3-6.htm); Pss. 115:4–5; 135:15–17; [Isa. 46:7](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/46-7.htm); [Jer. 10:5](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/10-5.htm), 14; [Rev. 12:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-11.htm); 14:7; 20:4.

### [Rev 13:16](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-16.htm) — "He causeth all … to receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads"

**Text**: "And he causeth all, both small and great, rich and poor, free and bond, to receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads:"

**Exegesis**: The threefold merismus ("small and great, rich and poor, free and bond") indicates **universal scope without social exception** — no class exemption. All must receive the mark. The earth beast causes (*poiei*) this — legislative and social enforcement.

**Mark** (Gk. *charagma*): literally an imprinted or engraved mark; also a graven image (cf. [Acts 17:29](https://biblehub.com/acts/17-29.htm)). *Charagma* was a technical term for:
- The imperial stamp on commercial documents
- The royal impression on Roman coins
- Branding of animals

Notably, ancient evidence does not show *charagma* being placed on human persons (slaves were branded with *stigmata*, cf. [Gal. 6:17](https://biblehub.com/galatians/6-17.htm)). This confirms the **symbolic** nature of the beast's mark: it is the identification mark of belonging to the satanic trinity, not a literal physical brand.

The **location** — right hand or forehead — mirrors the placement of the divine seal. The seal of God is placed "on the foreheads" of God's servants ([Rev. 7:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-3.htm); 9:4; 14:1). The mark of the beast on hand or forehead denotes the satanic ownership-claim that stands in opposition to God's seal. "The basic function of both the seal and the mark is ownership, identity, and protection. Both are thus signs of loyalty to God or the beast, respectively" (Stefanovic).

The mark of the beast = the name of the beast (v. 17; cf. [Rev. 14:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-11.htm)). The seal of God = the name of the Lamb and the Father ([Rev. 14:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-1.htm)). These are the two ultimate signs of eschatological allegiance.

What does the mark symbolize? Revelation 12–14 establishes that the end-time issue centers on **the commandments of God** as the standard of loyalty ([Rev. 12:17](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-17.htm); 14:12). The beast's attacks target specifically the first four commandments: (1) Demanding worship attacks "You shall have no other gods" (v. 4, 8). (2) Raising an image to be worshiped violates "You shall not make for yourself an idol" (vv. 14–15). (3) Blasphemy attacks "You shall not take the name of the LORD your God in vain" (vv. 5–6). (4) The mark of the beast is the direct attack on the **Sabbath commandment** — the fourth commandment as the seal of the Decalogue and the sign of covenant relationship ([Exod. 31:12](https://biblehub.com/exodus/31-12.htm)–17; [Ezek. 20:12](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/20-12.htm), 20). The Sabbath, as the creation-memorial ([Exod. 20:11](https://biblehub.com/exodus/20-11.htm)) and redemption-memorial ([Deut. 5:15](https://biblehub.com/deuteronomy/5-15.htm)), is the sign distinguishing God's covenant people. The mark of the beast functions as the **counterfeit Sabbath** — obedience to the beast's worship-day in place of obedience to God's Sabbath. As Johnsson: "The Sabbath, anciently the `sign' of the people of God ([Exod 31:13](https://biblehub.com/exodus/31-13.htm); [Ezek 20:20](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/20-20.htm)), will again come to the fore to show the world those who put God first."

**Cross-references**: [Exod. 20:8](https://biblehub.com/exodus/20-8.htm)–11; 31:12–17; [Deut. 5:15](https://biblehub.com/deuteronomy/5-15.htm); [Ezek. 20:12](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/20-12.htm), 20; [Gal. 6:17](https://biblehub.com/galatians/6-17.htm); [Rev. 7:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-3.htm); 9:4; 14:1, 9–11; 20:4.

### [Rev 13:17](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-17.htm) — "That no man might buy or sell"

**Text**: "And that no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark, or the name of the beast, or the number of his name."

**Exegesis**: The **economic boycott** is the mechanism of social enforcement. The inability to "buy or sell" without the mark imposes total economic exclusion on those who refuse — affecting food, shelter, commerce, livelihood. This is the end-time analog to social persecution, making nonconformity materially catastrophic. It mirrors the social pressure of the imperial cult in John's day (certificates of conformity required for commerce in emperor-worshiping cities), but the final application transcends that historical context.

**Textual note**: Manuscript variants affect the precise relationship between the terms. Codex Alexandrinus reads "the mark, that is, the name of the beast, or the number of his name" — placing the name in apposition to the mark, so that **the mark consists of the name of the beast**. This is Stefanovic's preferred reading. P47 (earliest Greek ms.) reads "the mark or the name of the beast or the number of his name" — treating the three as alternatives. Codex Sinaiticus reads "the mark of the beast or his name or the number of his name." The Alexandrinus reading is favored as placing mark and name in direct equivalence, creating a deliberate **contrast** with [Rev. 14:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-1.htm) — where the sealed saints have the name of the Lamb and the Father on their foreheads.

Three expressions refer to the same reality: the mark = the name of the beast = the number of his name. The mark is not merely an economic identifier; it is a statement of ultimate loyalty and identity.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 13:16](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-16.htm); 14:1, 9, 11; 16:2; 19:20; 20:4.

### [Rev 13:18](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-18.htm) — "Here is wisdom … his number is Six hundred threescore and six"

**Text**: "Here is wisdom. Let him that hath understanding count the number of the beast: for it is the number of a man; and his number is Six hundred threescore and six."

**Exegesis**: The call for "wisdom" (*sophia*) and "understanding" (*nous*) signals that this is not a simple puzzle but requires **divinely granted discernment** — the kind of wisdom that comes from God, not from human intellectual cleverness or calculation (cf. [Rev. 17:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/17-9.htm), same formula).

**Gematria**: Since the second century, interpreters have applied *gematria* — the technique (from Gk. *geometria*) of assigning numerical values to letters (A=1, B=2, etc.) — to decode 666. Proposed identifications include Nero Caesar (Gk. or Heb. transliteration), Caligula, Muhammad, Napoleon, Hitler, and the papal inscription *Vicarius Filii Dei*. Stefanovic rejects all gematria-based approaches for two reasons: (1) the technique's possibilities for 666 are virtually unlimited, yielding too many candidates; (2) John nowhere else in Revelation employs gematria for identification. When he describes the 144,000 as having the name of Christ and the Father "written on their foreheads" (14:3), he intends symbolic significance, not mathematical calculation.

**"A human number"** (Gk. *arithmos anthrōpou*): 666 is humanity's number — finite, creaturely, imperfect — as opposed to the divine number. The beast embodies what is human without God.

**Babylonian background**: Babylonian mathematics was sexagesimal (base 6 and 60). Six and sixty were significant in Babylonian religion — sixty was the number of their supreme god. Nebuchadnezzar's golden image ([Dan. 3:1](https://biblehub.com/daniel/3-1.htm)) was 60 cubits × 6 cubits. A Babylonian priestly amulet contained a magic square of numbers 1–36 whose rows and columns and diagonals all sum to 111, and whose total sum is 666. Six is thus the number of Babylon. It stands in contrast to the divine seven — the number of completeness and perfection (see Notes on [Rev. 5:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/5-1.htm)).

**Theological meaning of 666**: Six is one short of seven. Humanity was created on the sixth day — the apex of creation, yet not the fullness (which is the seventh-day Sabbath, the crown of creation). Six "finds its real meaning in seven when human beings lift themselves from the sphere of the mundane and consecrate themselves to God." The number 666 therefore, as Neall argues, "represents the refusal of man to proceed to seven, to give glory to God as Creator and Redeemer. It represents man's fixation with himself, man seeking glory in himself and his own creations ... The beasts of Rev. 13 represent man exercising his sovereignty apart from God, man conformed to the image of the beast rather than to the image of God."

Thus 666 = **the symbol of fullest human imperfection** — the ultimate expression of autonomous humanity, claiming divine honor, refusing the Sabbath-rest of consecration to God, exalting itself to divine status. The number cannot be broken by gematria but is apprehended only through divine discernment (*sophia*). 

Immediately following ([Rev. 14:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-1.htm)), John sees the 144,000 with the **name of the Lamb and the Father** on their foreheads — a deliberate contrast. If that name is a spiritual reality (not gematric), then 666 likewise is a spiritual reality, not a code to be cracked. The beast's number falls short of the divine character it counterfeits.

**Cross-references**: [Dan. 3:1](https://biblehub.com/daniel/3-1.htm); [Rev. 14:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-1.htm); 17:9; [2 Cor. 1:21](https://biblehub.com/2_corinthians/1-21.htm)–22; [Eph. 1:13](https://biblehub.com/ephesians/1-13.htm)–14; [Rev. 5:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/5-1.htm).

---

**Chapter Summary**: Revelation 13 presents the satanic Trinity assembled against the remnant (12:17). The **sea beast** (vv. 1–10): a composite of all Danielic empires (Dan. 7), receiving the dragon's power and throne, bearing blasphemous names, claiming divine prerogatives, exercising authority for forty-two months (the medieval period of religious oppression), wounding and recovering in a mock death-and-resurrection, winning worldwide worship through the deceptive question "Who is like the beast?" The **earth beast / false prophet** (vv. 11–18): lamb-like (Christlike in appearance), dragon-like in speech (the great deceiver), parody of the Holy Spirit who exalts not Christ but the sea beast, executing the satanic agenda through signs (counterfeit Elijah, counterfeit Pentecost), directing construction of the beast's image (counterfeit Daniel 3 enforced by civil power), and imposing the **mark of the beast** — the eschatological sign of loyalty to the satanic trinity in opposition to the seal of God, understood in Stefanovic's framework as the counterfeit Sabbath standing against God's Sabbath commandment as the ultimate test of loyalty in the final crisis. The number **666** encapsulates the theological reality: humanity exalting itself to divine status, refusing to give glory to the Creator, forever falling one short of the divine seven.
