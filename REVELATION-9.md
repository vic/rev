## Revelation 9

### [Rev 9:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-1.htm) — "And the fifth angel sounded..."

**Text**: "And the fifth angel sounded, and I saw a star fall from heaven unto the earth: and to him was given the key of the bottomless pit."

**Exegesis**: The "star fallen from heaven" (Gr. *astera peptōkota*, perfect tense) is crucial — the perfect tense indicates the star had already fallen *before* the fifth trumpet sounds. This is not a neutral celestial body but a personal agent. The star is identified later as "the angel of the abyss" (v. 11), and is the same "great star" of the third trumpet (8:10–11), there named Wormwood. The image evokes the fall of Satan ([Isa. 14:12](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/14-12.htm); [Luke 10:18](https://biblehub.com/luke/10-18.htm); [Rev. 12:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-7.htm)–10), one of the most glorious angels who rebelled against God. Collins notes that "the star falling from heaven to earth evokes the story of the fall of Satan." The star is thus Satan himself, now given the key of the "bottomless pit" (*abyssos*, abyss).

The *abyssos* first appears in [Genesis 1:2](https://biblehub.com/genesis/1-2.htm) (LXX) for the chaotic pre-creation deep; in [Jer. 4:23](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/4-23.htm)–30, for desolated Palestine during the Exile; and developed in Jewish apocalyptic as a subterranean prison for fallen angels and demons held under God's control ([Luke 8:31](https://biblehub.com/luke/8-31.htm); [2 Pet. 2:4](https://biblehub.com/2_peter/2-4.htm); Jude 6; [Rev. 20:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/20-1.htm), 3). In Revelation it is the origin of the beast (11:7; 17:8) and the place of Satan's millennial imprisonment (20:1–3).

The passive "was given" (*edothē*) is the *divine passive*, a convention common in Second Temple Judaism where God's name was not directly uttered; it makes clear that Satan does not independently possess this key — it is granted by God. [Rev. 1:18](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-18.htm) establishes that Christ holds the keys of Hades; the demonic forces operate only within divine permission. The unleashing of evil is simultaneously God's sovereign judgment and a controlled act.

**Cross-references**: [Isa. 14:12](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/14-12.htm); [Luke 10:18](https://biblehub.com/luke/10-18.htm); [Rev. 12:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-7.htm)–10 (Satan's fall); [Rev. 8:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-10.htm)–11 (third trumpet star); [Rev. 1:18](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-18.htm) (Christ holds keys of Hades); [Rev. 9:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-11.htm) (angel of abyss identified); [Gen. 1:2](https://biblehub.com/genesis/1-2.htm); 7:11 (abyss/deep); [Luke 8:31](https://biblehub.com/luke/8-31.htm); [2 Pet. 2:4](https://biblehub.com/2_peter/2-4.htm); Jude 6; [Rev. 20:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/20-1.htm), 3 (abyss as demonic prison).

### [Rev 9:2](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-2.htm) — "And he opened the bottomless pit..."

**Text**: "And he opened the bottomless pit; and there arose a smoke out of the pit, as the smoke of a great furnace; and the sun and the air were darkened by reason of the smoke of the pit."

**Exegesis**: The opening of the abyss releases a cloud of dense smoke, compared to "smoke of a great furnace" — an image with two strong OT antecedents: (1) the smoke ascending from the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah, described as going up "like the smoke of a furnace" ([Gen. 19:28](https://biblehub.com/genesis/19-28.htm)); (2) God's presence at Sinai, where smoke ascended "like the smoke of a furnace" ([Exod. 19:18](https://biblehub.com/exodus/19-18.htm)). Both are contexts of divine judgment. Smoke is a symbol of judgment throughout Revelation (cf. 14:11; 19:3).

The result is total darkening of the sun and air — an intensification of the *partial* darkness of the fourth trumpet (8:12). In the fourth trumpet, a third of the sun, moon, and stars was struck; here the darkness becomes complete, reminiscent of the Egyptian plague of darkness "so thick that people could not see each other or move from their place" ([Exod. 10:22](https://biblehub.com/exodus/10-22.htm)–23). The progression is deliberate: the darkening of spiritual sources of light (fourth trumpet = Enlightenment/secularism obscuring the gospel) gives way to the total spiritual darkness of demonic unleashing.

The exposition clarifies that the smoke is not mere atmospheric phenomenon: it is the gigantic cloud of demonic locusts blocking the sun, just as the locusts of the Egyptian plague "darkened the sky" ([Exod. 10:15](https://biblehub.com/exodus/10-15.htm)) and as Joel's locust plague caused the darkening of heavenly bodies ([Joel 2:2](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-2.htm)–10). Theologically, darkness here symbolizes the obscuring of the gospel; when people reject Christ, demonic forces fill the resulting spiritual vacuum.

**Cross-references**: [Gen. 19:28](https://biblehub.com/genesis/19-28.htm) (Sodom smoke); [Exod. 19:18](https://biblehub.com/exodus/19-18.htm) (Sinai smoke); [Exod. 10:22](https://biblehub.com/exodus/10-22.htm)–23 (Egyptian darkness); [Exod. 10:15](https://biblehub.com/exodus/10-15.htm) (locusts darkened sky); [Joel 2:2](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-2.htm)–10 (locust darkening); [Rev. 8:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-12.htm) (fourth trumpet, partial darkness); [Rev. 14:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-11.htm); 19:3 (smoke as judgment).

### [Rev 9:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-3.htm) — "And there came out of the smoke locusts upon the earth..."

**Text**: "And there came out of the smoke locusts upon the earth: and unto them was given power, as the scorpions of the earth have power."

**Exegesis**: The locusts emerge from the abyss-smoke, making clear the smoke and locusts are inseparable — the enormous cloud of locusts *is* the smoke, blocking the sun. Locusts in the OT are a standard symbol of divine judgment: against Babylon ([Jer. 51:14](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/51-14.htm)), against Egypt ([Exod. 10:4](https://biblehub.com/exodus/10-4.htm)–15), and against apostate Judah in Joel's vision of the day of the Lord ([Joel 2:4](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-4.htm)–10). However, these locusts are emphatically symbolic and demonic, not literal, for three reasons Stefanovic enumerates:

1. They have the power of scorpions — normal locusts do not sting.
2. They attack *people*, not plants — the reverse of real locust behavior.
3. Their description (armies, chariots, darkening sky) is drawn from Joel's vision, not entomology.

Their origin in the abyss, the prison of demonic forces, confirms they are demonic powers. The key hermeneutical anchor is [Luke 10:17](https://biblehub.com/luke/10-17.htm)–20, where Jesus declares: "I have given you authority to tread upon serpents and scorpions, and over all the power of the enemy" — using snakes and scorpions as explicit metaphors for demons and evil spirits. John clearly has this passage in mind; these locusts are what Paul calls "the power of darkness and wickedness" ([Eph. 6:12](https://biblehub.com/ephesians/6-12.htm)). They are demonic forces temporarily confined to the abyss and now unleashed.

The passive "was given to them" (*edothē autois*) — again the divine passive — marks God's sovereign control even over demonic power.

**Cross-references**: [Exod. 10:4](https://biblehub.com/exodus/10-4.htm)–15 (Egyptian locust plague); [Jer. 51:14](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/51-14.htm) (locusts against Babylon); [Joel 2:2](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-2.htm)–10 (locust vision, day of the Lord); [Luke 10:17](https://biblehub.com/luke/10-17.htm)–20 (scorpions = demons); [Eph. 6:12](https://biblehub.com/ephesians/6-12.htm) (powers of darkness); [Rev. 9:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-10.htm) (tails like scorpions).

### [Rev 9:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-4.htm) — "And it was commanded them that they should not hurt the grass..."

**Text**: "And it was commanded them that they should not hurt the grass of the earth, neither any green thing, neither any tree; but only those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads."

**Exegesis**: This verse inverts normal locust behavior (which destroys vegetation) and thus seals the symbolic interpretation. Grass and green trees in Scripture are recurring symbols for God's people (cf. [Ps. 1:3](https://biblehub.com/psalms/1-3.htm); [Rev. 8:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-7.htm) notes); the prohibition against harming vegetation here means the *sealed people of God are protected from demonic attack*. The demonic locusts can only harm those "who do not have the seal of God upon their foreheads" — the unsealed, those outside Christ's protection.

The sealing is the event of Revelation 7, where 144,000 servants of God are sealed on their foreheads. This creates a direct structural link between chapters 7 and 9: the restraint of ch. 7 (hold the winds until the sealing is complete) now has its consequence — the sealed are immune, the unsealed are exposed. Paul's assurance is the backdrop: "I have given you authority to tread upon serpents and scorpions... and nothing shall injure you" ([Luke 10:19](https://biblehub.com/luke/10-19.htm)). Those sealed by Christ are beyond the locusts' reach.

Theologically, the gospel functions as protection. The demonic torment falls on those who have rejected Christ; it is not arbitrary divine punishment but the consequence of choosing spiritual darkness. Those who are in Christ are sealed and shielded.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 7:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-1.htm)–8 (sealing of 144,000); [Rev. 8:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-7.htm) (grass as symbol of God's people); [Rev. 13:16](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-16.htm)–17 (mark of the beast as counterfeit seal); [Luke 10:19](https://biblehub.com/luke/10-19.htm) (authority over serpents/scorpions); [2 Tim. 2:19](https://biblehub.com/2_timothy/2-19.htm) (God knows his own).

### [Rev 9:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-5.htm) — "And to them it was given that they should not kill them, but that they should be tormented five months..."

**Text**: "And to them it was given that they should not kill them, but that they should be tormented five months: and their torment was as the torment of a scorpion, when he striketh a man."

**Exegesis**: A key restraint: the demonic locusts may *torment* but not *kill* (in contrast to the sixth trumpet, where killing occurs — v. 15, 18). The fifth trumpet plague is therefore not physical but *spiritual and mental*: it causes "unbearable psychological torture and suicidal anguish" (see v. 6). The scorpion sting is an apt image — scorpions (Barclay: lobster-like insects up to six inches, with poisonous curved tail-claw) deliver excruciating but usually non-lethal venom.

"Five months" (*mēnas pente*) has been variously proposed as: (a) the natural lifespan of locusts, meaning one full locust generation of harm; (b) a day-year calculation in historicist schemes. Stefanovic rejects both as "untenable and tentative" and instead finds the OT background in the Genesis Flood narrative — the *only* OT text mentioning a five-month period, and mentioned there twice ([Gen. 7:24](https://biblehub.com/genesis/7-24.htm); 8:3). The Flood lasted and harmed the earth for five months, during which Noah and his family were under divine protection. The parallel is exact: the demonic locusts harm the earth and its inhabitants for five months, while those sealed (like Noah's family) are specially protected.

**Cross-references**: [Gen. 7:24](https://biblehub.com/genesis/7-24.htm); 8:3 (five months of Flood); [Rev. 9:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-10.htm) (five months repeated); [Rev. 9:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-15.htm), 18 (sixth trumpet kills); [Job 3:21](https://biblehub.com/job/3-21.htm) (longing for death); [Jer. 8:3](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/8-3.htm) (choosing death over life).

### [Rev 9:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-6.htm) — "And in those days shall men seek death, and shall not find it..."

**Text**: "And in those days shall men seek death, and shall not find it; and shall desire to die, and death shall flee from them."

**Exegesis**: This verse describes the existential despair produced by demonic torment — not merely physical suffering, but the anguish of meaninglessness, spiritual emptiness, and alienation. The imagery has OT precedents: [Job 3:21](https://biblehub.com/job/3-21.htm) speaks of one who "longs for death that does not come"; [Jer. 8:3](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/8-3.htm) describes the day when men will choose death rather than life. The suicide wish that cannot be fulfilled dramatizes the severity: the torment is so intense that death would be a relief, yet even that escape is denied.

In Stefanovic's historicist/symbolic reading, this describes the condition of the secular world from the 18th century onward — the product of Enlightenment atheism and secularism. Traditional God-centered theology was replaced by man-centered philosophy (deism, relativism, nihilism, nationalism, communism). The resulting spiritual alienation — no God, no future, no meaning — produced the despair of secular humanity. The "seeking death" maps to the anguish of people who have separated themselves from God and find no adequate substitute. LaRondelle: "traditional God-centered theology was replaced by a man-centered philosophy, in which man is accountable only to himself."

The verse also echoes the broader Revelation theme: those without the seal of God have no protection and no comfort, while the sealed live in the hope of Christ.

**Cross-references**: [Job 3:21](https://biblehub.com/job/3-21.htm) (longing for death); [Jer. 8:3](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/8-3.htm) (choosing death over life); [John 3:18](https://biblehub.com/john/3-18.htm)–21 (rejection of light leads to darkness); [Rev. 6:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/6-9.htm)–10 (martyrs under altar crying "how long").

### [Rev 9:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-7.htm) — "And the shapes of the locusts were like unto horses prepared unto battle..."

**Text**: "And the shapes of the locusts were like unto horses prepared unto battle; and on their heads were as it were crowns like gold, and their faces were as the faces of men."

**Exegesis**: John now describes the locusts' appearance in elaborate, grotesque detail (vv. 7–10), consistently using simile language ("as," "like") — signaling symbolic, not literal, description. The primary OT source is [Joel 2:4](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-4.htm)–10, from which John draws most details and which he reconfigures for his own purpose. The Joel passage:

> "Their appearance is like the appearance of horses; And like war horses, so they run. With a noise as of chariots they leap on the tops of the mountains..."

**Horses prepared for battle**: [Joel 2:4](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-4.htm) directly — the imagery conveys power, discipline, and military precision. Natural locusts do resemble small horses (hence the German *Heupferd*, "hay-horse").

**Crowns like gold**: The *stephanos* (victor's wreath, here "as it were") signals their apparent authority and conquering posture; these are not laurel wreaths of human athletes but a demonic parody of divine authority.

**Faces like human faces**: Intelligence, purposefulness, and perhaps the ability to deceive — unlike mindless insects, these creatures have the appearance of rational agents.

Stefanovic notes that not every detail is necessarily to be allegorized; the grotesque composite description as a whole conveys the "bizarre character of evil powers and the awesomeness of their harm" and functions as a vivid warning.

**Cross-references**: [Joel 2:4](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-4.htm)–10 (primary OT source for vv. 7–10); [Rev. 6:2](https://biblehub.com/revelation/6-2.htm) (white horse, crown); [Rev. 9:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-8.htm) (lion teeth); Rev. 13 (beast composite imagery).

### [Rev 9:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-8.htm) — "And they had hair as the hair of women, and their teeth were as the teeth of lions."

**Text**: "And they had hair as the hair of women, and their teeth were as the teeth of lions."

**Exegesis**: Two further terrifying composite features, both drawn from Joel's locust vision:

**Hair as women's hair**: [Joel 1:6](https://biblehub.com/joel/1-6.htm) describes the locust nation whose "teeth are the teeth of a lion" and whose "fangs are the fangs of a lioness." Women's hair in the ancient world connoted length and wildness in a military context (Josephus notes Parthian cavalry were known for their long flowing hair, which some commentators have used to suggest Parthian imagery — though Stefanovic ultimately views this as symbolic rather than historical). In the composite creature, the hair adds to the chaotic, untamed, and terrifying aspect of these demonic forces.

**Teeth like lions**: [Joel 1:6](https://biblehub.com/joel/1-6.htm) directly — a symbol of devouring, destructive power. Lion teeth are the most feared natural weapon in the ancient Near East; Joel applies this image to the locust plague ravaging Judah. John intensifies the picture: these locusts are not passive consumers of vegetation but active, lion-fanged destroyers.

Together these features emphasize that these demonic forces combine seductive deceptive power (the feminine hair, evoking enticement) with brute destructive force (lion's teeth). The combination is ideologically significant: demonic activity operates through both deception and destruction.

**Cross-references**: [Joel 1:6](https://biblehub.com/joel/1-6.htm) (lion teeth applied to locusts); [Joel 2:4](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-4.htm)–10 (overall locusts-as-army imagery); [Rev. 9:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-7.htm) (horse-appearance, golden crowns); [Rev. 13:2](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-2.htm) (beast with lion's mouth).

### [Rev 9:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-9.htm) — "And they had breastplates, as it were breastplates of iron; and the sound of their wings was as the sound of chariots of many horses running to battle."

**Text**: "And they had breastplates, as it were breastplates of iron; and the sound of their wings was as the sound of chariots of many horses running to battle."

**Exegesis**: Two more Joelic elements:

**Iron breastplates**: Iron armor (*thōrakas sidērous*) symbolizes invulnerability — these demonic forces cannot be stopped by human resistance alone. The normal locust's scaly thorax carapace becomes, in the grotesque symbolism, iron plate armor. This communicates the futility of human opposition to demonic forces apart from divine protection.

**Sound of wings like chariots**: [Joel 2:5](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-5.htm) — "With a noise as of chariots they leap on the tops of the mountains, like the crackling of a flame of fire consuming the stubble." The auditory dimension of terror: the din of an approaching demonic army is like the thunder of war chariots and massed cavalry — overwhelming, unstoppable, announcing destruction. Chariots were the ancient world's most feared military technology (cf. Judg. 4–5; [Ps. 20:7](https://biblehub.com/psalms/20-7.htm)).

The whole composite picture of vv. 7–10 is deliberately intended, per Stefanovic, on two levels: (1) to portray the bizarre and awesome character of demonic evil; (2) to warn readers that the powers of darkness, long restrained, are now unleashed as never before in history.

**Cross-references**: [Joel 2:5](https://biblehub.com/joel/2-5.htm) (chariot-sound of locusts); [Rev. 9:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-7.htm) (breastplates shared with sixth-trumpet horsemen, 9:17); Judg. 4–5; [Ps. 20:7](https://biblehub.com/psalms/20-7.htm) (chariots in OT warfare).

### [Rev 9:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-10.htm) — "And they had tails like unto scorpions, and there were stings in their tails..."

**Text**: "And they had tails like unto scorpions, and there were stings in their tails: and their power was to hurt men five months."

**Exegesis**: The scorpion-tail motif returns, now clarified as the *mechanism* of the locusts' power to hurt. The tail (*ourā*) in Revelation is a rich theological symbol:

- [Isa. 9:14](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/9-14.htm)–15: the "tail" is the prophet who teaches lies — the false teacher who deceives the people. "The prophet who teaches lies, he is the tail."
- [Rev. 12:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-4.htm): the tail of the great red dragon (Satan) sweeps away a third of the stars of heaven — symbolizing the deception by which Satan misled the angels to rebel against God.
- [Rev. 13:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-13.htm)–14: false signs and deception.

The pattern is consistent: the tail is the symbol of *deception through false teaching and persuasion*, not physical violence. The demonic locusts torment people by their sting, but that sting operates through ideological deception — the corrupting philosophies and false religions that cause spiritual agony. The harm is "obviously not military, but rather ideological." The five-month duration (cf. v. 5) is reiterated to underscore the bounded, divinely-controlled nature of this plague.

**Cross-references**: [Isa. 9:14](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/9-14.htm)–15 (tail = false prophets); [Rev. 12:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-4.htm) (dragon's tail sweeps stars); [Rev. 13:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-13.htm)–14 (deception); [Rev. 9:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-5.htm) (five months, first mention); [Rev. 9:19](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-19.htm) (sixth trumpet tails like serpents with heads).

### [Rev 9:11](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-11.htm) — "And they had a king over them, which is the angel of the bottomless pit..."

**Text**: "And they had a king over them, which is the angel of the bottomless pit, whose name in the Hebrew tongue is Abaddon, but in the Greek tongue hath his name Apollyon."

**Exegesis**: This verse provides the decisive identification. In contrast to natural locusts, which have no king ([Prov. 30:27](https://biblehub.com/proverbs/30-27.htm)), these demonic locusts have a sovereign: the *angel of the abyss*. This figure is identified as the same fallen star of v. 1 — Satan himself.

**Abaddon** (Hebrew *'ăbaddōn*, "destruction/place of destruction"): In the OT, Abaddon refers to the realm of destruction associated with death and Sheol — the underworld of the dead ([Job 26:6](https://biblehub.com/job/26-6.htm); 28:22; [Ps. 88:11](https://biblehub.com/psalms/88-11.htm); [Prov. 15:11](https://biblehub.com/proverbs/15-11.htm); 27:20). John personalizes it: Abaddon becomes not a place but the *personification of destruction*, a proper name for Satan as the principle of annihilation.

**Apollyon** (Greek *Apollyōn*, "the Destroyer" or "the one who destroys"): The exact Greek equivalent of Abaddon. John provides both names — for his Jewish and Greek readers — making the identification unmistakable. The bilingual naming is also a literary device emphasizing that this is no ambiguous figure; the Destroyer is named in both languages of the biblical world.

The name fits Satan's character and role throughout Scripture: "The thief comes only to steal and kill and destroy" ([John 10:10](https://biblehub.com/john/10-10.htm)); he "prowls about like a roaring lion, seeking someone to devour" ([1 Pet. 5:8](https://biblehub.com/1_peter/5-8.htm)); he is "the ruler of the demons" ([Matt. 12:24](https://biblehub.com/matthew/12-24.htm); cf. [Matt. 25:41](https://biblehub.com/matthew/25-41.htm); [Rev. 12:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-9.htm)). His followers are "the destroyers of the earth" who will themselves be destroyed at the seventh trumpet ([Rev. 11:18](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-18.htm)). Satan's special work at this stage is organizing demonic forces for the decisive eschatological battle — which the sixth trumpet then begins to describe.

**Cross-references**: [Prov. 30:27](https://biblehub.com/proverbs/30-27.htm) (locusts have no king); [Job 26:6](https://biblehub.com/job/26-6.htm); 28:22; [Ps. 88:11](https://biblehub.com/psalms/88-11.htm); [Prov. 15:11](https://biblehub.com/proverbs/15-11.htm); 27:20 (Abaddon in OT); [John 10:10](https://biblehub.com/john/10-10.htm) (thief comes to destroy); [1 Pet. 5:8](https://biblehub.com/1_peter/5-8.htm) (roaring lion); [Matt. 12:24](https://biblehub.com/matthew/12-24.htm) (ruler of demons); [Rev. 12:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-9.htm) (Satan, old serpent); [Rev. 11:18](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-18.htm) (destroyers destroyed); [Eph. 2:2](https://biblehub.com/ephesians/2-2.htm) (prince of the power of the air).

### [Rev 9:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-12.htm) — "One woe is past..."

**Text**: "One woe is past; and, behold, there come two woes more hereafter."

**Exegesis**: A transitional verse marking the conclusion of the fifth trumpet (first woe) and warning that two woes remain. The three-woe announcement was first made by the eagle/vulture in 8:13 (*ouai, ouai, ouai*) over "the inhabitants of the earth" — those who dwell on earth as the opponents of God. The woe structure divides the seven trumpets: trumpets 1–4 are divine warnings with redemptive purpose (intended to bring the wicked to repentance); trumpets 5–7 (the three woes) are demonic judgments of escalating severity on those who have rejected the gospel.

The declaration functions as a pastoral warning to the reader: the fifth trumpet plague — with all its psychological torment and spiritual despair — is only the *beginning* of eschatological judgment. Two worse afflictions are imminent. This creates urgent expectation and serves the book's paraenetic purpose (to keep the faithful persevering). The "woe" language (*ouai*) is OT prophetic woe-oracle language (Isa. 5; Amos 5–6), applied here to the end-time condition of the wicked.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 8:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-13.htm) (eagle announcing three woes); [Rev. 11:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-14.htm) (second woe passes); [Rev. 11:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-14.htm)–15 (third woe — seventh trumpet); Isa. 5; Amos 5–6 (OT woe-oracle background).

### [Rev 9:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-13.htm) — "And the sixth angel sounded, and I heard a voice from the four horns of the golden altar..."

**Text**: "And the sixth angel sounded, and I heard a voice from the four horns of the golden altar which is before God,"

**Exegesis**: The sixth trumpet introduces the second woe. The voice issues not from the throne directly but from the *horns of the golden altar* — the altar of incense before the throne ([Rev. 8:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-3.htm)–4; cf. [Exod. 30:6](https://biblehub.com/exodus/30-6.htm): "in front of the veil... in front of the mercy seat... where I will meet with you"). The horns of the altar were the points of highest sanctity in the OT sanctuary ([Exod. 29:12](https://biblehub.com/exodus/29-12.htm); 30:10); blood was applied to them on the Day of Atonement.

This location is theologically loaded: the golden altar of incense is where, in 8:3–5, the prayers of the saints were offered before God and judgment was poured out in response. The voice from the altar's horns in the sixth trumpet explicitly connects this judgment to those intercessory prayers — the cry of the martyrs under the altar in the fifth seal (6:9–10: "How long, O Lord... will you not judge and avenge our blood?"). The sixth trumpet therefore represents an *advance in divine response to the prayers of God's oppressed people*, not an arbitrary cosmic event. The altar of incense is the instrument through which the saints' intercessions become divine action in history.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 8:3](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-3.htm)–5 (altar of incense, prayers of saints, judgment); [Rev. 6:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/6-9.htm)–10 (martyrs' cry for vindication); [Exod. 30:6](https://biblehub.com/exodus/30-6.htm) (altar before mercy seat); [Exod. 29:12](https://biblehub.com/exodus/29-12.htm); 30:10 (horns of altar, OT significance).

### [Rev 9:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-14.htm) — "Saying to the sixth angel which had the trumpet, Loose the four angels which are bound in the great river Euphrates."

**Text**: "Saying to the sixth angel which had the trumpet, Loose the four angels which are bound in the great river Euphrates."

**Exegesis**: The command is to release four angels "bound" (*dedemenous*) at the Euphrates — bound because they are destructive forces held in divine restraint. The Euphrates (*potamos ho megas*) is the key geographical symbol:

**OT significance**: Called "the great river" ([Gen. 15:18](https://biblehub.com/genesis/15-18.htm); [Deut. 1:7](https://biblehub.com/deuteronomy/1-7.htm); [Josh. 1:4](https://biblehub.com/joshua/1-4.htm)), the Euphrates marked the boundary separating Israel/God's people from their archenemies Assyria and Babylon ([Isa. 7:20](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/7-20.htm); [Jer. 46:10](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/46-10.htm)). Isaiah describes enemy invasion as "the waters of the Euphrates overflowing its banks and sweeping over the land of Judah, reaching up even to the neck" ([Isa. 8:7](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/8-7.htm)–8). In John's symbolic world, the Euphrates is the origin-point of the threat against God's people.

**Roman-era significance**: The Euphrates was the eastern boundary of the Roman Empire; beyond it lay the Parthian Empire, which defeated Roman armies three times (53 BC, 35 BC, AD 62), creating intense Roman anxiety about eastern invasion. Jewish apocalyptic texts applied Ezekiel's Gog prophecy (Ezek. 38–39) to the Parthians beyond the Euphrates. Many first-century readers would immediately think of Parthian cavalry. However, Stefanovic (with Beasley-Murray) argues this cannot be a literal Parthian invasion because it embraces the entire world and the army size (200 million) is literally impossible. John uses the Parthian imagery but transforms it into a supernatural, infernal host.

**Identity of the four angels**: These are the same four angels of [Rev. 7:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-1.htm)–3, who there held the four winds of earth to prevent harm until the sealing was complete. In ch. 9, they restrain the demonic army. The releasing of these angels (divine passive again) allows the demonic cavalry to begin their killing campaign.

**Cross-references**: [Gen. 15:18](https://biblehub.com/genesis/15-18.htm); [Deut. 1:7](https://biblehub.com/deuteronomy/1-7.htm); [Josh. 1:4](https://biblehub.com/joshua/1-4.htm) (Euphrates as "great river"); [Isa. 7:20](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/7-20.htm); [Jer. 46:10](https://biblehub.com/jeremiah/46-10.htm) (enemies from beyond Euphrates); [Isa. 8:7](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/8-7.htm)–8 (Euphrates flood = enemy invasion of Judah); Ezek. 38–39 (Gog invasion); [Rev. 7:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-1.htm)–3 (four angels holding winds); [Rev. 16:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-12.htm) (Euphrates again, sixth bowl).

### [Rev 9:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-15.htm) — "And the four angels were loosed, which were prepared for an hour, and a day, and a month, and a year, for to slay the third part of men."

**Text**: "And the four angels were loosed, which were prepared for an hour, and a day, and a month, and a year, for to slay the third part of men."

**Exegesis**: The four angels were "prepared" (*hētoimasmenoi*) — a divine passive showing God's sovereign pre-arrangement — for a specific moment: "the hour and day and month and year." This is best understood as a single divinely-appointed moment in time (a "divinely-appointed moment," per Stefanovic), not a cumulative duration. God has predetermined the precise instant of this release; the demonic forces have no freedom to act before the time set by God. This is a fundamental theological point: demonic activity is always within the boundaries of divine sovereignty.

The purpose of release is to "slay the third part of men" (*apokteinōsin to triton tōn anthrōpōn*). "A third" is the standard trumpet-plague fraction (cf. 8:7–12), indicating a large but not complete judgment — partial, leaving survivors who are then given opportunity to repent (cf. vv. 20–21). This contrasts with the fifth trumpet, where killing was prohibited; the sixth trumpet escalates to actual death. The demonic forces are given progressively greater freedom as history moves toward the end.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 8:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-7.htm)–12 (third-part judgments in first four trumpets); [Rev. 9:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-5.htm) (fifth trumpet: no killing); [Rev. 9:18](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-18.htm) (third part killed confirmed); [Rev. 16:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-12.htm)–16 (sixth bowl, Armageddon preparation — parallel to sixth trumpet).

### [Rev 9:16](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-16.htm) — "And the number of the army of the horsemen were two hundred thousand thousand: and I heard the number of them."

**Text**: "And the number of the army of the horsemen were two hundred thousand thousand: and I heard the number of them."

**Exegesis**: "Two hundred thousand thousand" (*dis myriades myriadōn* = literally "two myriads of myriads" = 20,000 × 10,000 = 200,000,000). This is a symbolic, not literal, number — representing an innumerable, overwhelming multitude, comparable to "the chariots of God are tens of thousands and thousands of thousands" ([Ps. 68:17](https://biblehub.com/psalms/68-17.htm)). No literal cavalry force of 200 million is in view.

The phrase "I heard the number of them" (*ēkousa ton arithmon autōn*) is a direct echo of [Rev. 7:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-4.htm): "I heard the number of those who were sealed" (*ēkousa ton arithmon tōn esphragismenōn*). This verbal parallel is not accidental: the 200 million demonic horsemen are the *demonic counterfeit* of the 144,000 sealed servants of God. Both groups are numbered; both are prepared for the final battle. The symbolic contrast is stark — God's sealed remnant (144,000) versus Satan's innumerable host (200 million). Paulien's structural observation: these are the only two places in Revelation containing the phrase "I heard the number" (*ēkousa ton arithmon*), making the literary link deliberate and theologically significant.

**Cross-references**: [Ps. 68:17](https://biblehub.com/psalms/68-17.htm) (innumerable chariots of God); [Rev. 7:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-4.htm) (144,000 sealed, "I heard the number"); [Dan. 7:10](https://biblehub.com/daniel/7-10.htm) (ten thousand times ten thousand before the throne — similar hyperbolic multitude); [Rev. 9:14](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-14.htm) (four angels released).

### [Rev 9:17](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-17.htm) — "And thus I saw the horses in the vision, and them that sat on them, having breastplates of fire, and of jacinth, and brimstone..."

**Text**: "And thus I saw the horses in the vision, and them that sat on them, having breastplates of fire, and of jacinth, and brimstone: and the heads of the horses were as the heads of lions; and out of their mouths issued fire and smoke and brimstone."

**Exegesis**: John describes the demonic cavalry in detail parallel to his description of the demonic locusts (vv. 7–10), again using simile ("as"). Three elements dominate:

**Breastplates of fire, jacinth (hyacinth-blue/smoky), and brimstone (sulphurous yellow)**: The three colors correspond precisely to the three substances issuing from the horses' mouths — fire (red/orange), smoke (dark blue/hyacinth), sulphur (yellow). The riders are, as it were, clothed in the materials of divine judgment. This is "materials from the lake of fire" ([Rev. 19:20](https://biblehub.com/revelation/19-20.htm); 20:10; 21:8), identifying the demonic army with the final place of punishment.

**Heads like lions**: While the fifth-trumpet locusts had *teeth* like lions (v. 8), the sixth-trumpet horses have *heads* like lions — an escalation in ferocity. The lion-head is the seat of the mouth, from which the deadly fire-smoke-sulphur issues.

**Fire, smoke, and sulphur from mouths**: This triadic combination is consistently the OT/NT symbol of God's judgment on the wicked: the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah ([Gen. 19:24](https://biblehub.com/genesis/19-24.htm), 28; [Luke 17:29](https://biblehub.com/luke/17-29.htm)); punishment of the wicked ([Ps. 11:6](https://biblehub.com/psalms/11-6.htm); [Isa. 34:9](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/34-9.htm)–10; [Ezek. 38:22](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/38-22.htm)); the torment of those who receive the mark of the beast ([Rev. 14:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-10.htm)–11); the lake of fire for Satan and his forces ([Rev. 20:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/20-10.htm); cf. 19:20; 21:8).

**Cross-references**: [Gen. 19:24](https://biblehub.com/genesis/19-24.htm), 28 (fire and sulphur on Sodom); [Ps. 11:6](https://biblehub.com/psalms/11-6.htm); [Isa. 34:9](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/34-9.htm)–10; [Ezek. 38:22](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/38-22.htm) (fire-smoke-sulphur as judgment); [Rev. 14:10](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-10.htm)–11 (mark-of-beast receivers tormented with fire and sulphur); [Rev. 19:20](https://biblehub.com/revelation/19-20.htm); 20:10; 21:8 (lake of fire).

### [Rev 9:18](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-18.htm) — "By these three was the third part of men killed, by the fire, and by the smoke, and by the brimstone..."

**Text**: "By these three was the third part of men killed, by the fire, and by the smoke, and by the brimstone, which issued out of their mouths."

**Exegesis**: The "three plagues" — fire, smoke, sulphur — are the agents of death for a third of humankind. Stefanovic identifies a *chiastic structure* in 9:17b–18:

> A from their mouths  
> B were coming out  
> C fire and smoke and sulphur.  
> D From these three plagues were killed the third part of humankind,  
> C' from the fire and the smoke and the sulphur  
> B' coming out  
> A' from their mouths.

This literary structure places "the third part of humankind killed" at the center (D), surrounded by the triple plague element, which itself is framed by the repeated "from their mouths." The chiasm underscores that the *mouth* is the decisive weapon — confirming that this killing is verbal/ideological in nature (see v. 19), not military.

The "third part" again signals partial judgment — serious but not total annihilation. Space remains for repentance (vv. 20–21 will note bitterly that the survivors do not repent). The fifth trumpet tormented; the sixth trumpet kills — escalating divine judgment as the end approaches.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 9:17](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-17.htm) (fire, smoke, sulphur described); [Rev. 8:7](https://biblehub.com/revelation/8-7.htm)–12 (third-part motif throughout trumpets); [Rev. 9:20](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-20.htm)–21 (survivors do not repent).

### [Rev 9:19](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-19.htm) — "For their power is in their mouth, and in their tails: for their tails were like unto serpents, and had heads, and with them they do hurt."

**Text**: "For their power is in their mouth, and in their tails: for their tails were like unto serpents, and had heads, and with them they do hurt."

**Exegesis**: The sixth-trumpet horsemen's power operates in *both* mouth and tail — an intensification from the fifth trumpet, where power was only in the tails. The dual weapons signify both killing (mouth: fire-smoke-sulphur) and torment/deception (tail: serpent-like).

**Tails like serpents with heads**: In [Rev. 12:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-9.htm), Satan is explicitly "the old serpent" (*ho ophis ho archaios*); his tail sweeps a third of the stars (12:4) — symbolizing deception of the angels. In [Isa. 9:14](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/9-14.htm)–15, the false prophet is "the tail." The serpent-tails having heads means they actively bite/strike with intelligent, directed deception — unlike the scorpion-tails of the fifth trumpet (which simply sting). The escalation is: scorpion-sting torment (fifth trumpet) → serpent-bite death (sixth trumpet).

**The mouth as spiritual weapon**: Stefanovic develops this at length. In Revelation, the mouth (*stoma*) is the primary instrument of spiritual warfare on both sides. Christ's sword proceeds from his mouth (1:16; 2:16; 19:15, 21). The two witnesses kill enemies with fire from their mouths (11:5). The dragon's mouth pours out water to destroy the woman (12:15). The sea beast's mouth speaks blasphemies (13:5–6). The three unclean frog-spirits come from the mouths of the dragon, beast, and false prophet to assemble the kings for Armageddon (16:13–14). The consistent pattern: "the battle between the forces of good and evil in the book of Revelation is verbal rather than physical in character." The demonic horses kill with their mouths — through false doctrine, deceptive ideology, and blasphemy, not swords.

**Cross-references**: [Rev. 12:9](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-9.htm) (Satan = old serpent); [Rev. 12:4](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-4.htm) (dragon's tail sweeps stars); [Isa. 9:14](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/9-14.htm)–15 (tail = false prophet); [Rev. 1:16](https://biblehub.com/revelation/1-16.htm); 2:16; 19:15, 21 (Christ's sword from mouth); [Rev. 11:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/11-5.htm) (two witnesses, fire from mouth); [Rev. 12:15](https://biblehub.com/revelation/12-15.htm) (dragon's mouth, water against woman); [Rev. 13:5](https://biblehub.com/revelation/13-5.htm)–6 (beast's mouth, blasphemy); [Rev. 16:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-13.htm)–14 (three frog-spirits from mouths); [2 Cor. 10:3](https://biblehub.com/2_corinthians/10-3.htm)–5 (spiritual warfare, weapons not of flesh); [Eph. 6:10](https://biblehub.com/ephesians/6-10.htm)–12 (armor of God).

### [Rev 9:20](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-20.htm) — "And the rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not of the works of their hands..."

**Text**: "And the rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not of the works of their hands, that they should not worship devils, and idols of gold, and silver, and brass, and stone, and of wood: which neither can see, nor hear, nor walk:"

**Exegesis**: The devastating conclusion: those who survived the killing of the sixth trumpet — "the rest of humankind" — refused to repent. The purpose of divine judgment in the trumpet series has been redemptive as well as retributive; the plagues are meant to drive people to repentance (cf. the OT prophet pattern: judgment → call to return → hope of restoration). The first four trumpets were explicitly redemptive warnings. But the survivors of the demonic slaughter harden further in their sin.

They continue to worship two categories:

1. **Demons** (*daimonia*): The idols are explicitly linked to demons — behind the idol is the demon (cf. [1 Cor. 10:20](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/10-20.htm): "the things which the Gentiles sacrifice they sacrifice to demons").

2. **Idols of gold, silver, brass, stone, wood — which cannot see, hear, or walk**: The language is borrowed directly from [Daniel 5:23](https://biblehub.com/daniel/5-23.htm), where Belshazzar worshiped idols of silver and gold, bronze, iron, wood, and stone "which do not see, hear or understand," resulting in the fall of Babylon. Also [Ps. 115:4](https://biblehub.com/psalms/115-4.htm)–7: "Their idols are silver and gold... They have mouths but cannot speak, eyes but cannot see..." The idols' total sensory and motor incapacity — the sharpest possible contrast to the living God — makes the worship of them the paradigm of irrational rebellion.

Paul diagnoses these same sins in [Romans 1:18](https://biblehub.com/romans/1-18.htm)–32 as the product of idolatry: when humanity suppresses the truth about God, they are given over to degrading passions and a debased mind. The sins listed in v. 21 (murders, sorceries, fornication, thefts) are Paul's catalog of the fruit of idolatry.

**Cross-references**: [Dan. 5:23](https://biblehub.com/daniel/5-23.htm) (idols that cannot see/hear/walk, fall of Babylon); [Ps. 115:4](https://biblehub.com/psalms/115-4.htm)–7 (mute idols); [1 Cor. 10:20](https://biblehub.com/1_corinthians/10-20.htm) (sacrifices to demons); [Rom. 1:18](https://biblehub.com/romans/1-18.htm)–32 (idolatry and its vices); [Rev. 21:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/21-8.htm); 22:15 (same sins in final judgment).

### [Rev 9:21](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-21.htm) — "Neither repented they of their murders, nor of their sorceries, nor of their fornication, nor of their thefts."

**Text**: "Neither repented they of their murders, nor of their sorceries, nor of their fornication, nor of their thefts."

**Exegesis**: Four vices — *phonos* (murder), *pharmakeia* (sorcery/magic arts), *porneia* (sexual immorality/fornication), *klopē* (theft) — represent the full moral collapse of a society that has rejected God. Paul describes these sins as the necessary product of idolatry ([Rom. 1:18](https://biblehub.com/romans/1-18.htm)–32; [Gal. 5:19](https://biblehub.com/galatians/5-19.htm)–21). They appear again in [Rev. 21:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/21-8.htm) and 22:15 as the defining characteristics of those excluded from the New Jerusalem.

**Pharmakeia** (sorcery) is particularly significant: it refers not just to magic arts but to drug-use in occult contexts, the manipulation of spiritual forces, and the counterfeit spirituality that fills the void left by rejection of the gospel. In the fifth trumpet's reading, this connects to the "New Age movement" and the growing activities of false spirituality that rush in when the gospel is rejected.

The fourfold list covers violence (murder), false spirituality (sorcery), sexual sin (fornication), and economic sin (theft) — a comprehensive survey of human transgression in all its dimensions: against life, against the divine, against the body, against property. The repetition of "did not repent" (*ou metanoesan*) is the chapter's darkest note. The purpose of the woe-plagues — to drive the wicked to repentance (cf. [2 Pet. 3:9](https://biblehub.com/2_peter/3-9.htm): "The Lord is not slow... but is patient toward you, not wishing that any should perish, but that all should come to repentance") — has completely failed for this group.

This failure of repentance signals the approaching end of intercession. The sixth trumpet brings into view the preparation for Armageddon ([Rev. 16:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-12.htm)–16). As Desmond Ford writes: "The multitudes who have rejected the blood of the atonement, the incense of Christ's righteousness, the refreshment from the divine rivers and fountains, and the light from heavenly orbs, have no protection against the doctrine of devils, and ultimately no protection against the devils themselves." The interlude of Rev. 10–11:14 and the three angels of [Rev. 14:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-6.htm)–13 represent God's final merciful proclamation of the everlasting gospel before the seventh trumpet closes the door.

**Cross-references**: [Rom. 1:18](https://biblehub.com/romans/1-18.htm)–32 (vices as fruit of idolatry); [Gal. 5:19](https://biblehub.com/galatians/5-19.htm)–21 (works of the flesh); [Rev. 21:8](https://biblehub.com/revelation/21-8.htm); 22:15 (same vices in eschatological judgment); [2 Pet. 3:9](https://biblehub.com/2_peter/3-9.htm) (God's patience, not wishing any to perish); [Rev. 16:12](https://biblehub.com/revelation/16-12.htm)–16 (sixth bowl, Armageddon — parallel end-point); Rev. 10–11:14 (interlude: everlasting gospel before seventh trumpet); [Rev. 14:6](https://biblehub.com/revelation/14-6.htm)–13 (three angels, final gospel proclamation); [Luke 13:3](https://biblehub.com/luke/13-3.htm), 5 (the wicked will perish if they refuse to repent).

**Structural note on Chapter 9**: The chapter divides into two trumpet-woe units (fifth trumpet = first woe, vv. 1–12; sixth trumpet = second woe, vv. 13–21) with a clear theological escalation: torment without death (fifth) → death plus torment (sixth). Both units share demonic forces, horse imagery, tail symbolism, and divine-passive authorization. The chapter ends not with the destruction of the wicked but with their hardened impenitence — making the succeeding interlude (chs. 10–11) the final merciful divine effort before the seventh trumpet concludes the series. The parallel between [Rev. 7:1](https://biblehub.com/revelation/7-1.htm)–8 (sealing of 144,000; "I heard the number") and [Rev. 9:13](https://biblehub.com/revelation/9-13.htm)–21 (four angels released; 200 million demonic counterparts; "I heard the number") is the chapter's master structural key: two armed and numbered hosts — God's sealed people and Satan's demonic army — face off in preparation for the eschatological final battle.
