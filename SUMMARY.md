# Revelation of Jesus Christ — Stefanovic Commentary

## Meta

- **Source**: Ranko Stefanovic commentary on Revelation
- **Author**: John (apostle, son of Zebedee)
- **Written**: Patmos, reign of Domitian (AD 81-96)
- **Recipients**: Seven churches, Roman province of Asia Minor

---

## Purpose

- Comfort Christians under:
  - Emperor worship demands
  - Roman persecution
  - Internal heresy/apostasy
  - Jewish opposition/synagogue expulsion
  - Trade guild economic pressure
- Reveal Christ's post-Calvary ministry
- Assure God controls history

---

## Interpretive Frameworks

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Preterism | Only 1st century; no predictive prophecy |
| Idealism | Timeless truths; no historical application |
| Futurism | End-times only |
| Historicism | Christian history from John to end |

**Commentary position**: Text-governed; combines elements; recognizes both historical and future prophecy.

**Hermeneutic**:
1. Historical context (1st-century Asia)
2. Timeless application

**Key rule**: OT governs symbol interpretation (278/404 verses reference OT). Bible interprets Revelation. Christ = center of all prophecy.

---

## Symbolic Sources

1. **OT** (68.7% of verses): Creation, Flood, Exodus, Davidic Covenant, Babylon exile
2. **Jewish apocalyptic**: 1 Enoch, 4 Ezra, 2 Baruch
3. **1st-century Asia Minor**: Cultural/religious practices
4. **NT parallels**: Gospel and epistle themes

## Literary Structure

### Threefold (based on 1:19; 11:19)

```
A. Prologue (1:1-8)            — Divine origin & theme
B. Seven Churches (1:9-3:22)   — Christ as High Priest
C. Sealed Scroll (4-11:19)     — Christ as Eschatological Ruler
   - Enthronement (4-5)
   - Seven Seals (6-8:1)
   - Seven Trumpets (8:2-11:18)
   - Covenant Appearance (11:19)
D. Scroll Contents (12-22:5)   — Christ as Apocalyptic Michael
   - Dragon & Woman (12)
   - Two Beasts (13)
   - Final Gospel (14)
   - Seven Bowls (15-18)
   - Final Events & New Jerusalem (19-22:5)
E. Epilogue (22:6-21)          — Promise & Call
```

### Sanctuary Foundation
- First half (1-11): Daily temple service (Tamid)
- Second half (12-22:5): Annual Yom Kippur service

### Annual Festival Pattern
- **Passover** (1-3): Death & resurrection
- **Pentecost** (4-5): Christ's inauguration; Spirit outpouring
- **Feast of Trumpets** (8-9): Call to prepare
- **Day of Atonement** (11-22:5): Judgment & final atonement
- **Feast of Tabernacles** (21-22:5): Celebration in renewed earth

## Seven Churches

### Format (sixfold)
1. Address: "To the angel of the church..."
2. Christ's self-description (from Rev. 1)
3. Appraisal: "I know..."
4. Counsel: repent or endure
5. Appeal: "He who has an ear..."
6. Promise to overcomer

### Interpretive Levels
- Local churches (specific)
- Universal church (general)
- Historical periods (prophetic)

### Theological Patterns
- Spiritual decline across messages
- Promises *increase* as churches decline (grace abounds where sin increases — Rom 5:20)
- "Overcome" (Gk: *nikao*) = continuous victory; all can participate

## Seven Churches: Detail

### 1. Ephesus (2:1-7)
- **Problem**: Lost first love → legalism without love
- **Counsel**: Remember, repent, do first works
- **Warning**: Lampstand removed
- **Promise**: Tree of life in paradise
- **Historical**: Apostolic period

### 2. Smyrna (2:8-11)
- **Condition**: Materially poor, spiritually rich
- **No rebuke**
- **Trial**: Emperor worship, Jewish persecution; "ten days" = short period
- **Promise**: Crown of life; escape second death
- **Historical**: 2nd-3rd century Roman persecution

### 3. Pergamum (2:12-17)
- **Problem**: Tolerating Nicolaitans/Balaamites (compromise with paganism)
- **Issues**: Idol food, cultic prostitution
- **Warning**: Christ wars with sword of mouth
- **Promise**: Hidden manna, white stone, new name
- **Historical**: Post-Constantine; church won vs. paganism but compromised spiritually

### 4. Thyatira (2:18-29)
- **Positive**: Love, faith, service, perseverance; last works > first
- **Problem**: Tolerating "Jezebel" (false prophet advocating compromise)
- **Deep things of Satan**: Claimed full knowledge of sin = greater appreciation of grace
- **Promise**: Authority over nations; morning star (Christ himself)
- **Historical**: Middle Ages (6th-16th century)

### 5. Sardis (3:1-6)
- **No commendation**
- **Problem**: Reputation for life but spiritually dead
- **Counsel**: Watch, remember, repent
- **Promise**: White garments, name not blotted, confessed before Father
- **Historical**: Protestant scholasticism (16th-17th century); lifeless formalism

### 6. Philadelphia (3:7-13)
- **No rebuke**
- **Condition**: Little strength but kept word
- **Promise**: Open door, kept from hour of trial, pillar in God's temple, God's name written on them
- **Key**: "Kept from" = protected *during* trial (cf. John 17:15), not removed before
- **Historical**: 18th-19th century Protestant revival; great missionary advance

### 7. Laodicea (3:14-22)
- **Problem**: Lukewarm — neither cold nor hot; self-sufficient
- **Reality**: Wretched, miserable, poor, blind, naked
- **Three prescriptions**: Refined gold (faith), white garments (salvation), eye salve (discernment)
- **Door**: Christ knocks continually; personal choice required
- **Promise**: Sit on throne with Christ (incorporates all prior promises)
- **Historical**: Last period of earth's history

### Promise Escalation Summary
```
Ephesus    → tree of life
Smyrna     → crown of life + escape second death
Pergamum   → hidden manna + white stone + new name
Thyatira   → authority over nations + morning star
Sardis     → walk with Jesus + white clothes + name kept + acknowledged before Father
Philadel.  → kept from trial + pillar + never leave + names written
Laodicea   → sit on Christ's throne (all others included)
```

## Internal Church Threats (1st Century)

1. Food offered to idols (pagan feast participation)
2. Sexual immorality/cultic prostitution
3. False teaching: Nicolaitans, Balaamites, "Jezebel"
4. Emperor worship demands
5. Jewish opposition (synagogue expulsion)
6. Trade guild economic sanctions
7. Spiritual decline / loss of first love

### Heresy Core (Nicolaitans/Balaamites)
- Christian liberty = freedom from moral law
- Perverted Paul's teaching on grace
- "Prudent conformity" to pagan practices
- Claimed "deep knowledge of Satan" justified sin
- **Counter**: Separation ≠ isolation; different allegiance. Council of Jerusalem (Acts 15) binding.

## Enthronement of Christ (Rev. 4-5)

### When
- After ascension, at Pentecost (Acts 2:32-36; Eph. 1:20-22; Heb. 1:3)
- "Already and not yet": Christ reigns now; final subjugation future (1 Cor. 15:24-28)

### NOT a Judgment Scene
- No judgment language in chs. 4-5
- Key terms (worthy, Lion of Judah, right hand) = royalty
- Joyful adoration ≠ judgment tone
- Only one scroll; judgment books opened only at Rev. 20:11-15
- Taking (not opening) of scroll causes worship

### The Sealed Scroll
- Covenant Book (Deuteronomy pattern)
- Presented at coronation; token of right to rule
- Sealed at Exile due to Israel's unfaithfulness (Isa. 8:16; Dan. 12:9)
- Christ the only one worthy to take and open it

## Seven Seals (6-8:1)

### Four Horsemen (6:1-8)
| Horse | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| White | Gospel proclamation |
| Red | Division/conflict from gospel rejection |
| Black | Spiritual famine |
| Pale | Death (sword, famine, pestilence, beasts) |

- Represent general realities from cross to Second Coming
- Final phase: latter rain initiates final gospel proclamation (cf. Rev. 14:6-12)

### Fifth Seal (6:9-11)
- Martyrs under altar (blood = life poured as sacrifice)
- Plea for vindication (legal, not vengeance)
- Response: white robes, wait until fellow servants complete
- OT: Abel's blood (Gen. 4:10)

### Sixth Seal (6:12-17)
- Cosmic signs: earthquake, darkened sun, blood moon, falling stars
- Historical identifications: May 19, 1780 (darkening); Nov. 13, 1833 (meteor shower)
- Response of wicked: hide from "wrath of the Lamb"

### Seventh Seal (8:1)
- Silence in heaven (half hour = symbolic short period)
- Calm after Second Coming destruction; expectation before final judgment

## Sealed Saints & Great Multitude (Rev. 7)

### The Sealing (7:1-8)
- Four winds = destructive agents restrained
- Seal = Holy Spirit's ownership/protection (Eph. 1:13-14; 4:30)
- **144,000** = 12 × 12 × 1,000; spiritual Israel (not literal ethnic)
- Dan and Ephraim omitted (apostate tribes); Judah first (Messiah's tribe)

### Great Multitude (7:9-17)
- Same group as 144,000, different perspective (what John hears vs. sees)
- Pattern: hear (144k) → see (multitude); same as lion (5:5) → Lamb (5:6)
- White robes + palm branches = triumph (Maccabean; John 12:13)
- Passed through great tribulation; washed robes in Lamb's blood

### Promise to Multitude
- Before throne, serving day/night
- God's tent over them; no hunger/thirst/sun/heat
- Lamb shepherds to springs of life
- God wipes every tear (Isa. 25:8)

## Seven Trumpets (Rev. 8-11)

### Nature
- Response to fifth seal martyrs' plea (6:10)
- God remembers saints; sends warnings
- Target: "those who dwell on earth" = apostates (not humanity generally)
- Period: cross to Second Coming (parallel to seals)

### Introductory Scene (8:2-6)
- Prayers of saints presented with incense before throne
- Censer filled with altar fire, cast to earth = God's judgment response to prayer

### First Four Trumpets

| Trumpet | Events | OT Parallel | Historical Meaning |
|---------|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1st | Hail, fire, blood; 1/3 earth/trees/grass burned | 7th Egyptian plague | Fall of Jerusalem (AD 70) |
| 2nd | Burning mountain into sea; 1/3 sea→blood, ships destroyed | Jer. 51 (Babylon) | Fall of Rome; barbarian invasions |
| 3rd | Star "Wormwood" poisons rivers | Satan (Isa. 14:12) | Dark/Middle Ages apostasy; tradition replaces Scripture |
| 4th | 1/3 sun/moon/stars darkened | Isa. 13:10; Joel 2:10 | Age of Enlightenment; secularism obscures gospel |

### Fifth Trumpet (9:1-12)
- Fallen star (Satan) opens abyss; demonic locusts emerge
- Cannot harm sealed; torment unsealed 5 months (= Genesis Flood period)
- King: Abaddon/Apollyon (Destroyer)
- Historical: Atheistic philosophy; nihilism; demonic counterfeits

### Sixth Trumpet (9:13-21)
- Four angels released at Euphrates; army of 200 million
- Euphrates = boundary between God's people and enemies
- Spiritual warfare: mouth = weapon (ideology, not military)
- Historical: Preparation for Armageddon; final demonic intensification
- Survivors refuse to repent (9:20-21)

### Trumpets vs. Bowls Comparison

| Feature | Trumpets | Bowls |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Intercession | In progress | Completed; temple closed |
| Scope | Partial (1/3) | Global; no restriction |
| Purpose | Redemptive; call to repent | Punitive; no repentance produced |
| Duration | Long periods | Very short |
| Period | Cross to Second Coming | Immediately before Second Coming |

### Jericho Parallel
- Seven priests, trumpets, 7 days → city falls on 7th
- Bowls = part of seventh trumpet blast = God's response to nations' anger (11:18)

## Interlude: Little Scroll (Rev. 10)

### Strong Angel (10:1-7)
- Clothed in cloud; rainbow over head; face like sun; feet like fire
- Right foot on sea; left on land = universal message
- Seven thunders sealed (some mysteries remain with God — Deut. 29:29)
- Oath: "No more delay" — mystery of God completed at 7th trumpet
- Mystery = God's entire redemptive purpose (sealed in ch. 5; now completing)

### Eating the Scroll (10:8-11)
- Sweet as honey (joy of word) → bitter in stomach (opposition/disappointment)
- OT parallels: Jer. 15:16; 20:7-8; Ezek. 2:10-3:4
- Commission: prophesy again to many nations (final gospel — Matt. 24:14)
- **Adventist interpretation**: 1844 Great Disappointment → commission to "prophesy again"

## Two Witnesses (Rev. 11:1-14)

### Measuring (11:1-2)
- Temple (*naos*) = heavenly sanctuary (consistent usage throughout Revelation)
- Measuring = judicial evaluation
- Outer court excluded = court of Gentiles; given to nations
- Forty-two months = same as 1,260 days = "time, times, half time" (Dan. 7:25)
- Prophetic period ≈ 1,200 years (Medieval Age); not literal

### Identity of Witnesses
- **Bible** (OT + NT): Two witnesses follow Deut. 19:15 law of witnesses
- **God's people**: Primary task = witnessing (John 15:27; Acts 1:8)
- Combined: people of God in kingly/priestly function bearing witness to Word

### Powers
- Fire from mouth (Jer. 5:14; 2 Kings 1:9-14)
- Shut heaven: no rain (Elijah pattern — 1 Kings 17:1)
- Waters to blood (Moses pattern — Exod. 7-11)

### Death, Resurrection, Ascension
- Beast from abyss kills after 1,260 days
- Bodies exposed 3.5 days in "great city" = Sodom + Egypt + Jerusalem = apostate system
- Nations celebrate (exchange gifts like Purim — Esther 9:22)
- Resurrection: breath of life enters (Gen. 2:7; Ezek. 37); ascend in cloud (Acts 1:9)
- Earthquake; 7,000 killed; survivors give glory to God

## Seventh Trumpet (11:15-19)

- "Kingdom of world become kingdom of Lord and Christ" (Ps. 2:2; Dan. 2:44)
- Events: nations enraged → God's wrath → dead judged → servants rewarded → destroyers destroyed
- Temple opened; ark revealed; theophany signs
- Marks beginning of Rev. 12-22:5 (contents of little scroll)

## Dragon & Woman (Rev. 12)

### The Woman (12:1-2)
- = People of God (not Mary)
- OT: Zion/Israel as woman (Isa. 54:5-6; Jer. 3:20)
- NT: Church as bride (Eph. 5:25-32)
- Clothed with sun; moon under feet; 12 stars (Gen. 37:9-10)
- Crying = persecution/suffering, not ordinary labor

### The Dragon (12:3)
- = Satan (12:9), "ancient serpent" (Gen. 3)
- Red = bloodshed; 7 heads + 10 horns = kingdoms/powers through history
- Tail dragged 1/3 stars = fallen angels (Rev. 12:7-9)

### The Male Child (12:5)
- = Jesus Christ (only possible identification)
- Ps. 2:7-9: shepherd nations with rod of iron
- Caught up to God's throne (emphasis on exalted Christ, not earthly ministry)

### Wilderness Flight (12:6, 14)
- 1,260 days / "time and times and half a time" = Medieval persecution period
- God provides and protects (Exodus parallel)

### Satan's Flood (12:15-16)
- Flood = persecution OR deceptive teaching (not mutually exclusive)
- Earth swallows river = God protects from both force and deception

### The Remnant (12:17)
- Greek *loipos* = surviving faithful when majority apostatizes
- Two marks:
  1. Keep commandments of God
  2. Have testimony of Jesus (= possess prophetic gift; Rev. 19:10)

## Two Beasts (Rev. 13)

### Sea Beast (13:1-10)
- From sea = social/political upheaval; same as Daniel 7
- Composite: leopard + bear + lion = successor to all prior empires
- 7 heads + 10 horns; receives power from dragon (parody of Christ-Father)
- **Mortal wound** (*sphazó* = slaughter; same word as "slain Lamb"): parody of Christ's death/resurrection
- 42 months dominion; blasphemes God; wars against saints
- **Historical**: Medieval ecclesiastical power 1,200 years; mortal wound via Reformation/Enlightenment/French Revolution
- **Future**: Mortal wound healed; 8th head phase; universal end-time religious-political oppression

### Earth Beast / False Prophet (13:11-18)
- From earth (cf. Rev. 12:16)
- Two horns like lamb = Christlike appearance; speaks like dragon = deceptive
- Functions as parody of Holy Spirit (executes first beast's authority)
- **Activities**:
  1. Makes earth worship first beast
  2. Performs great signs (fire from heaven — parody of Pentecost/Elijah)
  3. Makes image of beast → gives it breath → image kills non-worshipers (Dan. 3 parallel)
  4. Enforces mark: buy/sell only with mark on hand or forehead
- **Historical**: Arises after sea beast wounded; exclusively end-time (no ancient antecedent)
- **Likely**: United States (lamb-like tolerance → dragon-like coercion in final crisis)

### Mark of Beast vs. Seal of God

| Feature | Seal of God | Mark of Beast |
|---------|------------|---------------|
| Location | Forehead | Forehead or hand |
| Meaning | Ownership by God | Ownership by Satan |
| Basis | Holy Spirit / character | Allegiance to beast's system |

- Mark = counterfeit Sabbath; human command substituted for God's
- Beast attacks 4 commandments: worship (1st), image (2nd), blasphemy (3rd), mark/Sabbath (4th)

### 666 (13:18)
- "Human number"; falls short of divine 7
- Babylonian sexagesimal math; 6 = Babylonian number
- Triple 6 = parody of divine Trinity (Father/Son/Spirit)
- = Unregenerate humanity seeking glory apart from God
- Requires Holy Spirit-enlightened discernment, not mathematical calculation

## Revelation 14: Gospel & Harvest

### 144,000 on Mt. Zion (14:1-5)
- Father's name + Christ's name on foreheads = sealed people
- New song = song of redemption (only redeemed can learn)
- Follow Lamb wherever he goes
- Firstfruits = sealed remnant, distinct from unbelieving nations

### Three Angels' Messages (14:6-12)
| Angel | Message |
|-------|---------|
| 1st | Eternal gospel to all nations; "Fear God, give him glory, worship Creator" |
| 2nd | "Babylon is fallen" (preview of Rev. 17-18) |
| 3rd | Warning against beast/image/mark; torment for worshipers |

### End-Time Saints (14:12)
- Keep commandments of God
- Hold faith of Jesus (*objective genitive* = faith IN Jesus)
- Called to endurance, not resistance

### Second Beatitude (14:13)
- "Blessed are dead who die in the Lord from now on"
- Rest (*kopos* = weariness-level labor) from hardship
- Contrast: beast-followers "have no rest day and night" (14:11)
- Works follow them = their endurance/obedience recorded

### Two Harvests (14:14-20)

#### Harvest of Righteous (14:14-16)
- "One like Son of Man" on white cloud with sickle = Christ (Dan. 7:13)
- Angel from temple commands: "Hour to reap has come"
- Metaphor: harvest gathers people into kingdom (Matt. 13:39-43; John 4:35-38)
- Timing: after sealing complete, probation closed, destiny fixed

#### Harvest of Wicked (14:17-20)
- Angel with sickle gathers grapes into winepress of God's wrath
- OT: winepress = executing divine wrath (Isa. 63:1-6; Joel 3:13)
- "Outside the city" = outside New Jerusalem
- Blood up to horse bridles; 1,600 stadia = hyperbolic; full overthrow of church's enemies

## Seven Last Plagues (Rev. 15-16)

### Nature
- God's final wrath in full strength (14:10); "last ones" — wrath reaches completion (15:1)
- Unlike trumpets (partial, redemptive): bowls = complete, punitive
- Timing: after every eternal destiny fixed (15:8); temple closed, intercession ended

### Exodus Motif
- Four bowl plagues parallel Egyptian plagues
- Sixth/seventh: modeled on fall of Babylon
- Christ = new Moses executing plagues on spiritual "Egypt"

### Trumpets vs. Bowls (recap)
- Trumpets: forewarning and foretaste
- Bowls: final judgment; wicked still refuse repentance (16:9, 11, 21)

### Structural Parallel

| # | Trumpets | Bowls |

| Intro | Temple scene (8:2-6) | Temple scene (15:5-16:1) |

| 6 | Euphrates (9:14-21) | Euphrates (16:12-16) |
| 7 | "Kingdom come" (11:15-16) | "It is done" (16:17-21) |

## Babylon (Rev. 17-18)

### The Prostitute (Rev. 17)
- Sits on many waters = peoples/nations/tongues (17:15)
- = End-time apostate religious system seducing political powers
- Beast phases: was (past) → is not (wounded/dormant) → will come (8th head revival)
- Seven heads = seven successive world powers (Egypt, Assyria, Babylon, Persia, Greece, Rome, future)
- Ten horns = ten end-time kings who surrender power to beast

### The Reversal (17:16-17)
- Ten kings initially support Babylon → realize deception → destroy her
- Drying Euphrates = withdrawal of political support
- **Divine control**: God puts purpose in their hearts (17:17); they unknowingly execute judgment

### Judgment of Babylon (Rev. 18)
**Three-part**:
1. Call to separation (18:1-8): "Come out of her, my people" (Jer. 51:6)
2. Laments of partners (18:9-19): kings, merchants, shipmasters mourn lost wealth
3. Heavenly rejoicing (18:20-24): saints vindicated; blood of prophets avenged

**Double recompense** (18:6): "Pay her double" = full/exact equivalent; punishment fits crime (Exod. 22:4-9)

**Symbolic action** (18:21): Millstone into sea = complete, irreversible destruction (Jer. 51:59-64)

**Babylon's crimes**:
1. Merchandised false doctrine ("great men of earth")
2. Deceived all nations by sorcery (*pharmakeia*)
3. Shed blood of prophets and saints

## Revelation 19: Two Suppers

### Wedding Supper of the Lamb (19:1-10)
- "Hallelujah!" — Hebrew praise; 4x in NT, 24x in Psalms
- **Reasons for joy**: God's righteous judgments; Babylon's permanent destruction
- **Hebrew wedding pattern**: Betrothal → groom prepares place → bride prepares → groom returns to claim
  - Christ betrothed church at Calvary; returned to Father's house (John 14:1-3); returns at Second Coming
- **Bride's garment**: Fine linen = righteous deeds of saints; *given* by Christ (grace, not self-merit)
- 4th Beatitude: "Blessed are those invited to wedding supper"

### The Warrior Christ (19:11-16)
- Faithful and True; eyes like flame; many crowns
- **Unknown name** (19:12) = likely "King of kings and Lord of lords" (19:16)
- **Robe dipped in blood** (before battle begins) = blood of persecuted witnesses (Isa. 63:1-6)
- **Word of God** (19:13): Creator (John 1:1) + prophetic assurance embodied
- Heavenly army = glorified saints; fine linen (same as bride)

### The Battle (19:17-21)
- Angel invites birds to feast (Ezek. 39:17-21)
- Beast + false prophet captured → lake of fire burning with sulphur
- Answers 13:4 question: "Who can wage war with the beast?" = the Lamb
- Rest killed by sword from Christ's mouth

### Destruction Order (reverse of introduction)
1. Babylon (17-18)
2. Beast + false prophet (19:20)
3. Dragon/Satan (20:10)

## Millennium (Rev. 20)

### Satan's Binding (20:1-3)
- Bound = deprived of activity; abyss = desolate, depopulated earth
- Living wicked destroyed at Second Coming; earth emptied
- Satan cannot deceive — no one left to deceive
- OT: Isa. 24:21-22; Jer. 4:23-26 (formless/void parallels)

### Three Millennial Views

| View | Timing | First Resurrection | Where Saints Reign |
|------|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Postmillennialism | Second Coming after 1,000 yrs | — | Earth |
| Amillennialism | 1st-2nd coming period | Spiritual rebirth / souls in heaven | Church on earth |
| Premillennialism (Adventist) | Second Coming before 1,000 yrs | Bodily at Second Coming | Heaven |

### Redeemed During Millennium (20:4-6)
- First resurrection at Second Coming (1 Thess. 4:16)
- Reign with Christ in heaven (Rev. 7:9-17; 19:1-10)
- Serve as priests and judges (Matt. 19:28; 1 Cor. 6:2)
- Purpose: examine God's dealings; understand fairness of divine justice
- Rest of dead (wicked) remain dead until millennium ends

### Satan's Release & Defeat (20:7-10)
- Second resurrection = wicked raised; earth repopulated
- Gog & Magog = rebellious nations hostile to God (Ezek. 38-39)
- Surround beloved city (New Jerusalem descended — 21:2)
- Fire from heaven devours them — no actual battle
- Satan cast into lake of fire; shares beast and false prophet's fate
- "Forever and ever" = action until God's purpose complete; not necessarily unending duration

### Final Judgment (20:11-15)
- Great white throne; earth and heaven flee
- All resurrected; no exceptions (sea, Death, Hades give up dead)
- Books of deeds opened + Book of Life
- Basis: works as evidence of heart loyalty/disloyalty
- Lake of fire = metaphor for complete destruction (Death and Hades also thrown in = figurative)
- Second death = final end; death itself destroyed (1 Cor. 15:26)

## New Jerusalem (Rev. 21-22:5)

### New Heaven & New Earth (21:1)
- *Kainos* = qualitatively new, not recently made
- Re-creation of existing elements, not ex nihilo
- Sea is no more = end of hostile forces; end of suffering and exile

### The Holy City (21:2-3)
- Descends from heaven; prepared by Christ (John 14:1-3)
- Actualized on earth after millennium
- "Tabernacle of God among men" = permanent, unmediated divine presence
- No separate sanctuary needed — city has all temple characteristics

### Restoration Promises (21:4-5)
- No death, sorrow, crying, pain (Isa. 25:8; 35:10)
- "I am making all things new" (Isa. 43:18-19)
- Creation freed from corruption (Rom. 8:21)

### Overcomer's Inheritance (21:6-7)
- Fulfills all seven churches' promises (Rev. 2-3)
- Freely from fountain of water of life
- Becomes God's child/heir

### Excluded (21:8)
- Cowardly, faithless, abominable, murderers, fornicators, sorcerers, idolaters, liars
- Second death = lake of fire

### City Description (21:9-22:5)

**Exterior**:
- Radiance like jasper (God's glory)
- Wall: 12 gates (12 tribes — OT) + 12 foundation stones (12 apostles — NT)
- 12,000 stadia (≈1,400 miles); cube shape = Holy of Holies (1 Kings 6:20)
- 144 cubits wall (12²); precious stones parallel high priest's breastplate (Exod. 28:17-20)
- Gates: twelve pearls; streets: pure gold transparent glass

**Interior** (21:22-22:5):
- No temple (*naos*): Lord God Almighty and Lamb ARE temple
- God's glory = light; Lamb = lamp; no sun/moon needed
- Gates never closed; no night; nothing unclean enters

**River of Life** (22:1-2):
- Crystal-bright; flows from throne of God and Lamb
- OT parallels: Eden (Gen. 2:9-10); Ezekiel's temple river (Ezek. 47:12)
- Spiritual meaning: Holy Spirit (Isa. 44:3-4; John 7:38-39)

**Tree of Life** (22:2):
- Both sides of river; 12 fruits monthly; leaves heal nations
- Healing = emotional/spiritual healing from exile's wounds; end of all suffering

**Eternal Condition** (22:3-5):
- No curse (Gen. 3:17-19 reversed)
- God's name on foreheads (= owned, protected; cf. mark of beast)
- No night; no need of lamp or sun
- Servants reign forever

## Key Doctrinal Themes

### Sealing vs. Mark
- Seal: Holy Spirit ownership; on forehead (mind/allegiance)
- Mark: Satan's ownership; on forehead (belief) or hand (forced compliance)
- Final crisis = worship test (14:7 vs. 13:8)

### Two Peoples
- 144,000 = church militant (before tribulation)
- Great multitude = church triumphant (after tribulation)
- Same group; different perspective

### Judgment Structure
- Seals: consequences of gospel rejection
- Trumpets: God's warning response to prayer; calls to repentance
- Bowls: final irrevocable punishment after probation closes

### Liberation Motif
- God delivers new-covenant remnant from apocalyptic Egypt and Babylon
- Takes them to New Jerusalem

### Prayer Foundation
- Entire trumpet series = God's answer to oppressed saints' prayers
- Human wickedness not unnoticed in heaven

### Great Controversy Theme
- **Rev. 17-18**: Satan's counterfeit city; deception
- **Rev. 19:11-21**: Christ destroys political/religious opposition
- **Rev. 20:1-10**: Satan bound; final defiance; final defeat
- **Rev. 20:11-15**: All rebels condemned; evil eradicated
- **Rev. 21-22**: Eternal union of God and redeemed; paradise restored

### Correct-by-Construction Principle
- God never loses control; permits what serves ultimate justice
- Ten kings unknowingly execute God's will (17:17)
- Satan's kingdoms destroy themselves; God's kingdom endures
